# Competitive Battle Card

**What it is:** An internal sales enablement document structured as a presentation — equipping reps with the positioning, objection handling, and tactical guidance needed to win against a specific competitor.

---

## Audience
Sales team: AEs, SDRs, and sales management. Internal reference used in deal coaching and competitive evaluations. Content should be usable mid-deal — specific, actionable, and quick to navigate.

## Tone
Direct and practical. This is not marketing copy — write it like you're briefing a rep 10 minutes before a call. Honest about where you lose, specific about where you win.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Your company vs. [Competitor]. Date (keep battle cards dated — they go stale).
2. **When You'll See Them** — What deals they appear in, what triggers their involvement.
3. **Their Pitch (Be Honest)** — What the competitor says about themselves. Their genuine strengths.
4. **Their Weaknesses** — Where they fall short. Customer-reported pain points. Back with evidence.
5. **How We Win** — Your differentiators in this match-up. Specific to this competitor.
6. **Head-to-Head Comparison** — Side-by-side table on dimensions that matter in this deal type.
7. **Common Objections + Responses** — "But [Competitor] has X..." — with your response.
8. **Landmines to Plant** — Questions to ask the prospect that expose competitor weaknesses.
9. **Proof Points + References** — Customers who switched from this competitor, competitive win stories.
10. **Trap Questions They Ask** — Questions the competitor's reps will ask prospects about you. Your response.
11. **Win Checklist** — If all of these are true, we should win this deal. Diagnostic.
12. **Escalation** — When to bring in leadership. What competitive deal support is available.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Honest assessment of competitor strengths — reps need to know what they're up against
- Objection handling with specific language (not just "emphasize our strengths")
- Landmines to plant — questions that expose competitor gaps without naming them
- Win rate context: current win rate vs. this competitor
- Dated: battle cards over 6 months old should be refreshed
- Proof points: win stories or customers who switched

## Must-Avoid
- Battle cards that make you look perfect and competitors look terrible — reps won't trust them
- Objections without specific responses
- Head-to-head tables that only compare where you win
- Battle cards not tied to a specific competitor (generic is useless)
- Strategies that require prospects to accept your framing before they can work

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Win rate vs. this competitor (overall and by deal size)
- Competitor pricing (if known)
- Common deal sizes where you compete head-to-head
- Number of customers who switched from this competitor
- Key differentiator with evidence (benchmark, test result, customer quote)

---

## Example Outline (Project Management vs. Asana)

1. Altura vs. Asana — Sales Battle Card (Updated Q2 2025)
2. When Asana Shows Up: Deal Size, Buyer Profile, Trigger Events
3. Asana's Genuine Strengths: Brand Recognition, UX, Ecosystem
4. Where Asana Falls Short: Jira Integration, Reporting at Scale, Seat Cost
5. How We Win: Native Jira Sync, Report Builder, Volume Pricing
6. Head-to-Head: 6 Key Dimensions
7. Common Objections: "Asana has better UI," "We already use Asana for X"
8. Questions to Ask: "How many teams are managing cross-tool dependencies?"
9. Win Stories: 3 Companies Who Moved from Asana to Altura (With Results)
10. Trap Questions Asana Reps Ask — And How to Respond
11. Win Checklist: If These 5 Things Are True, We Should Win
12. Escalation: When to Loop in SE or Sales Leadership
