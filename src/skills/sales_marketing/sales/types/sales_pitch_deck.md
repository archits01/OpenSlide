# Sales Pitch Deck

**What it is:** A structured buyer-facing deck that tells the company story, defines the problem, presents the solution, and makes a direct case for purchase.

---

## Audience
VP Operations, VP Customer Experience, or equivalent operational decision-makers at mid-market companies. Budget-holders who care about ROI, efficiency, and reducing friction — not technical depth.

## Tone
Confident, outcome-driven, consultative. Lead with their pain, not your product. Every slide should make the buyer think "that's exactly our problem." Let data do the persuading, not hype.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, one-line value proposition, presenter name, date
2. **The Problem** — Quantify the pain. Use industry benchmarks or data they recognize.
3. **Why Now** — Market shift, rising cost, or competitive pressure that makes this urgent
4. **How It Works** — Product/service in 3 steps or fewer. No jargon.
5. **Key Capabilities** — 3–4 features tied directly to the problems on slide 2
6. **Proof / Results** — Named customers or anonymized case studies with hard metrics
7. **ROI / Business Case** — What a typical customer gains. Payback period or cost avoidance.
8. **How We Work Together** — Onboarding timeline, implementation effort, support model
9. **Pricing** — Range or model. Don't hide it.
10. **Why Us vs. Alternatives** — Honest comparison. What you win on.
11. **Next Steps** — Single clear CTA: schedule a pilot, start a trial, sign an NDA

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Quantified customer outcome (e.g. "34% reduction in WISMO contacts")
- Before/after metrics from a real or representative customer
- Time-to-value statement (how fast do they see results?)
- Named or credible social proof (logo, quote, or anonymized reference)
- Implementation effort slide — buyers fear disruption
- A specific next step, not "any questions?"

## Must-Avoid
- Opening with company history or founding story — start with the buyer's problem
- Feature lists without outcomes
- Vague claims: "industry-leading," "best-in-class" — prove it or cut it
- More than 4 bullet points per slide
- Hiding pricing — signals lack of confidence
- Ending on Q&A with no CTA

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Customer improvement: key metric before and after (% change)
- Time-to-value / implementation timeline (weeks)
- ACV or pricing range
- Number of customers / logos
- NPS or retention rate if strong
- Industry benchmark to frame the problem magnitude
