# Case Study Presentation

**What it is:** A buyer-facing presentation of a customer success story — designed to build credibility in a competitive evaluation by showing a comparable customer's results in a relatable context.

---

## Audience
CISO, technical evaluators, procurement lead, or C-suite in an active competitive evaluation. They've heard vendor pitches — a credible customer story with hard results is more persuasive than any product claim.

## Tone
Evidence-led, specific, and credible. The story should feel like a genuine account of what happened — not a marketing brochure. Numbers matter more than adjectives.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Your company name, case study title, customer name (or anonymous descriptor)
2. **Why We're Sharing This** — Brief framing: why this customer is relevant to them (industry, size, problem type).
3. **Customer Context** — Who the customer is (industry, size, team), their role, their environment.
4. **The Challenge** — What problem they were facing before. Specific, quantified, and relatable.
5. **Why They Chose Us** — What made them select your solution over alternatives. Be honest — include what they considered.
6. **The Approach** — What was implemented, how long it took, what the rollout looked like.
7. **The Results** — Specific, quantified outcomes. Primary metric first, then secondary.
8. **Before / After Comparison** — Side-by-side: key metric before vs. after. Visual is more persuasive than text.
9. **What Changed for the Team** — Qualitative impact: time saved, confidence gained, process simplified.
10. **Customer Quote** — Direct quote if available. Attributed as specifically as possible.
11. **Relevance to Your Situation** — Draw the direct parallel: "Your environment has the same characteristic. Here's why we expect similar results."
12. **Next Steps** — Propose a reference call with this customer (if available) or a pilot.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Quantified results — primary metric with before/after numbers
- Customer context that's clearly comparable to the prospect (industry, size, problem)
- An explicit "why this is relevant to you" closing argument
- A reference call offer if the customer has agreed to speak
- The competitive context: what alternatives the customer considered

## Must-Avoid
- Case studies with vague outcomes ("significantly improved," "major time savings")
- Stories where the customer is too different from the prospect to be relatable
- Presenting the case study as a product features tour
- Customer quotes that are generic ("We love working with [Company]")
- Closing without connecting the story back to the prospect's specific situation

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Customer: company type, size (employees or revenue), team size
- Primary metric: % improvement or absolute change
- Timeline: time from deployment to results
- Scale: volume processed, users, data covered
- Competitive alternative considered and why they didn't choose it

---

## Example Outline (Cloud Security — Financial Services)

1. How [Financial Services Company] Reduced Critical Vulnerabilities by 67% in 90 Days
2. Why This Matters for Your Evaluation
3. Customer Context: 3,200-Person Financial Services Firm, 3 Cloud Environments
4. The Challenge: 8,400 Open Vulnerabilities, No Prioritization Framework
5. Why They Chose Wiz: Coverage, Agentless Architecture, Time-to-Value
6. The Implementation: Deployed in 4 Days, Full Coverage in 2 Weeks
7. The Results: 67% Critical Vulnerability Reduction in 90 Days
8. Before / After: Risk Posture Dashboard, Critical Alerts
9. What Changed for the Security Team: From Reactive to Proactive
10. What Their CISO Said
11. Why This Applies to Your Environment
12. Would You Like to Speak With Them Directly?
