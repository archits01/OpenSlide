# Sales Enablement Program Presentation

**What it is:** An internal presentation proposing or reviewing a sales enablement programme — covering the business case for investment, programme design, expected outcomes, and budget ask.

---

## Audience
CRO, VP Sales, or CFO. Approving budget and headcount for a structured enablement function. Audience is skeptical of enablement ROI — show them the productivity math, not the learning theory.

## Tone
Commercial and metrics-driven. Translate enablement work into quota attainment. Speak the CRO's language: ramp time, quota attainment rate, win rate, deal velocity. Frame every programme element as a lever on one of these numbers.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, programme title, budget request, presenter
2. **The Business Problem** — Current attainment, ramp time, win rate vs. target. The gap that enablement closes.
3. **What's Causing the Gap** — Root causes: ramp too slow, competitive positioning weak, objection handling inconsistent.
4. **What Other Companies See** — Benchmark data: industry ramp time, attainment rate, win rate. Where you stand.
5. **The Proposed Programme** — 3–4 workstreams. Each with goal and expected impact on a commercial metric.
6. **Workstream 1: New Hire Ramp** — Current: X months, Target: Y months. How we'll close the gap.
7. **Workstream 2: Competitive Positioning** — Win rate vs. top 3 competitors. Battlecard + training plan.
8. **Workstream 3: Objection Handling** — Common objections that lose deals. Playbook + coaching.
9. **Workstream 4: Ongoing Skill Development** — Cadence, format, measurement.
10. **The Investment** — Total budget, headcount required, tool costs.
11. **Expected ROI** — Ramp reduction = X additional quota-days. Attainment uplift = $Y incremental revenue.
12. **Timeline** — Programme phases and milestones.
13. **The Ask** — Budget approval, hire headcount, programme launch date.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Current attainment, ramp time, and win rate — the baseline the programme improves
- Root cause analysis — not just "reps need training"
- ROI model: ramp reduction → revenue impact, attainment uplift → revenue impact
- Programme elements each tied to a commercial metric they improve
- Budget breakdown: internal FTE, external vendor, tools
- A timeline with milestones for measurement

## Must-Avoid
- Enablement programmes built around learning formats ("we'll do workshops, e-learning, coaching")
- ROI claims without the math
- Proposals that don't address the CRO's top metric
- Generic "best practices" rather than company-specific diagnosis
- Budget asks without expected revenue impact

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Current quota attainment: % of reps at/above quota
- Current ramp time: months to full productivity
- Win rate: overall and vs. key competitors
- Sales team size: number of AEs and SDRs
- Budget ask: total and breakdown
- Expected improvement: ramp (months saved), attainment (% increase)

---

## Example Outline (B2B SaaS — Construction Tech)

1. Sales Enablement Programme 2025 — $340K Budget Request
2. The Problem: 67% Attainment, 6-Month Ramp — We're Leaving $X on the Table
3. Root Causes: Slow Ramp, Weak Competitive Positioning, Inconsistent Objection Handling
4. Where We Stand vs. Benchmarks (SaaS Sales Benchmarks)
5. The Programme: 4 Workstreams Over 12 Months
6. Workstream 1: Reduce Ramp from 6 to 4 Months
7. Workstream 2: Competitive Positioning — Win Rate vs. Procore, Autodesk, Buildertrend
8. Workstream 3: Objection Handling Playbook — 12 Deal-Killer Objections
9. Workstream 4: Monthly Skill Sprints + Call Coaching Cadence
10. Investment: $340K — 1 Enablement Hire + Tools + External Vendor
11. Expected ROI: 2-Month Ramp Reduction = $480K Additional Quota, 5% Attainment Lift = $720K
12. Timeline + Launch Plan
13. The Ask: Programme Approval + Hire Authorization
