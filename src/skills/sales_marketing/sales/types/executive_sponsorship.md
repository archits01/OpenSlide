# Executive Sponsorship / CFO Presentation

**What it is:** A targeted presentation to a C-suite executive (typically CFO or CEO) to establish executive sponsorship for a stalled deal — making the business case in their language and connecting the investment to their priorities.

---

## Audience
CFO, CEO, or COO. They weren't involved in the evaluation — their champion brought them in. They're skeptical, time-constrained, and will evaluate in terms of financial return, strategic fit, and risk.

## Tone
Executive-level, brief, and commercially precise. No product features. No technical depth. ROI, payback period, strategic alignment, and risk reduction — in that order. Treat them as a CFO, not a champion.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Your company name, meeting title (e.g. "Business Case for [Product]"), date
2. **Why We're Here** — Context: the evaluation in progress, what's been assessed, why executive alignment matters now.
3. **The Business Problem** — What problem this solves in their organization. Quantified. In their terms.
4. **What We've Proposed** — Solution overview in 2–3 bullets. Non-technical.
5. **The Investment** — Total cost, payment structure, term.
6. **Expected Return** — Revenue uplift, cost reduction, or risk mitigation. Quantified. Source your assumptions.
7. **Payback Period** — When does the investment break even? Graph it if helpful.
8. **3-Year Value Model** — NPV or simple cumulative return. Show the 3-year picture.
9. **Risk of Doing Nothing** — Cost of delay or inaction. Make it tangible.
10. **What's Been Validated** — What your champion and their team have confirmed. Shows this isn't theoretical.
11. **What We Need From You** — Specific ask: budget approval, executive sign-off, greenlight for phase 1.
12. **Appendix** — Detailed financial model, assumptions, technical due diligence summary (if relevant).

---

## Must-Have Elements
- ROI model with stated assumptions — not just a number
- Payback period calculation
- Risk of inaction / cost of delay
- What the champion team has already validated — reduces CFO's need to re-evaluate
- A specific ask with a decision timeline
- Non-technical framing throughout — no product jargon

## Must-Avoid
- Product feature slides — the CFO didn't ask to evaluate the product
- ROI claims without sourced assumptions
- Technical deep dives that weren't requested
- Leaving the ask implicit — "we hope you can support this" is not an ask
- Presentations longer than 10 slides (CFO time is extremely limited)

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Total investment: £/$ over contract term
- Expected return: £/$ annual or total 3-year
- Payback period: months
- NPV or ROI % at 3 years
- Cost of delay: £/$ per month of inaction
- Champion team validation: what they tested and confirmed

---

## Example Outline (Data Analytics — UK Retail Bank CFO)

1. Business Case for [Platform] — CFO Session
2. Why We're Here: £340K Decision With Executive Implications
3. The Problem: 3-Day Reporting Cycle Costs £X in Decision Lag
4. What We've Proposed: Enterprise Data Analytics Platform
5. The Investment: £340K ARR, 3-Year Term
6. Expected Return: £1.2M Annual in Analyst Efficiency + Decision Speed
7. Payback: 3.4 Months
8. 3-Year Value: £3.2M Net Return on £1.0M Investment
9. Risk of Inaction: £100K/Month in Delayed Decisions During Platform Delay
10. What Your Team Has Already Validated: POC Results + Security Review
11. The Ask: Budget Approval for Phase 1 by [Date]
12. Appendix: Financial Model + Assumptions
