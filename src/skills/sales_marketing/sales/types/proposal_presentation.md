# Proposal Presentation

**What it is:** A formal presentation of a commercial proposal to senior decision-makers — restating the problem, presenting the proposed solution, quantifying the expected return, and making a clear ask.

---

## Audience
C-suite (COO, CFO, CEO) and operational leadership. Evaluating a significant investment and likely comparing multiple vendors or options. Audience is sophisticated — they will stress-test your assumptions.

## Tone
Executive-level, commercially rigorous, and confident. Don't sell — advise. Use their language (ROI, payback period, risk reduction). Position as a partner who understands the business, not a vendor pitching features.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Your firm name, client name, proposal title, date
2. **The Situation** — Your understanding of their current state. Confirm before proceeding.
3. **What We Heard** — Pain points from discovery conversations. Shows you listened.
4. **The Problem in Numbers** — Quantify the cost of the current state: operational waste, revenue risk, inefficiency.
5. **Our Recommended Approach** — Proposed scope, methodology, and phases. Not a feature list — a plan.
6. **What's In Scope / Out of Scope** — Explicit. Prevents scope creep and sets expectations.
7. **Investment** — Total fee, payment structure, timeline. Clear.
8. **Expected Return** — Quantified: cost savings, revenue uplift, risk reduction. Show the math.
9. **Payback Period** — When do they break even? What's the 3-year value?
10. **How We'll Work Together** — Team, governance, reporting cadence, escalation path.
11. **Our Track Record** — 1–2 relevant case studies with outcomes comparable to this engagement.
12. **Why Now** — Cost of delay. What's the business case for starting now vs. waiting.
13. **The Ask** — Specific decision requested. Sign-off level, date, next steps.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- A quantified problem statement — "the cost of inaction is X"
- Explicit scope: in and out
- Investment + expected return on the same slide or back-to-back
- Payback period calculation
- Relevant case study with financial outcomes
- An explicit ask with a decision date

## Must-Avoid
- Proposals that lead with your firm's credentials before defining their problem
- Scope that's vague enough to include everything
- ROI claims without stated assumptions
- Case studies that are irrelevant to this client's industry or problem size
- Ending with "any questions?" instead of a specific ask

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Total investment: £/$ and payment terms
- Expected savings or revenue uplift: range (base/upside)
- Payback period: months
- Engagement duration: months
- Team: named consultants (lead partner, delivery lead)
- Comparable client outcomes: metric achieved

---

## Example Outline (Management Consulting — Food Manufacturing)

1. Operational Transformation Programme — Proposal for [Client]
2. The Situation: 1,400-Person Operation with Efficiency Challenges
3. What We Heard: 3 Pain Points From Our Discovery
4. The Problem in Numbers: £X Annual Waste from Inefficiency
5. Our Recommended Approach: 6-Month Operational Transformation
6. Scope: What's In and What's Out
7. Investment: £840K Over 6 Months — Phase Breakdown
8. Expected Return: £2.8M Annual Savings
9. Payback: 3.6 Months
10. How We Work Together: Team, Governance, Reporting
11. Track Record: How We Saved £3.1M for a Similar Manufacturer
12. Why Now: Cost of 6-Month Delay = £1.4M in Foregone Savings
13. The Ask: Programme Approval by [Date]
