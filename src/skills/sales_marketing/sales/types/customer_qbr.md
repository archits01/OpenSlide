# Customer QBR (Quarterly Business Review)

**What it is:** A structured review meeting with an existing customer — showing value delivered, identifying gaps, aligning on next-quarter priorities, and securing renewal or expansion.

---

## Audience
VP Operations, Head of HR, or equivalent customer-side decision-maker plus their team. They want to see that the investment is working. They're evaluating whether to renew, expand, or raise concerns.

## Tone
Consultative, data-driven, forward-looking. Don't just report what happened — analyze it. Come with recommendations. The QBR is a partnership meeting, not a vendor update.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Your company name, customer name, quarter, date
2. **Agenda** — What we'll cover. Reserve time for their items.
3. **Our Shared Goals** — What we agreed to achieve together. Remind them of their success criteria.
4. **Performance vs. Goals** — Metrics achieved vs. targets. Be honest about misses.
5. **Key Wins This Quarter** — 2–3 specific outcomes that happened. Names and numbers.
6. **Usage Deep Dive** — Adoption rate, active users, feature utilization. Compare to healthy benchmark.
7. **Where We Fell Short** — Gaps in adoption, support issues, unresolved items. Acknowledge and own.
8. **Roadmap Update** — What's coming that's relevant to them. Connect to their stated needs.
9. **Recommendations for Next Quarter** — 2–3 specific actions to improve outcomes. Your suggestions, not just asks.
10. **Expansion Opportunity (if applicable)** — New module, seats, or use case. Frame as solving a named problem.
11. **Renewal / Commercial** — Contract renewal timeline. Any pricing or terms discussion.
12. **Action Items + Owners** — Both sides. Leave with a signed-off list.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Performance vs. goals — both what was achieved and what wasn't
- Usage data with benchmark context (e.g. "67% adoption vs. 80% target for companies at your stage")
- At least one honest acknowledgment of a gap or miss
- Expansion opportunity framed as solving their problem, not your upsell target
- Action items with named owners from both sides
- Renewal timeline surfaced explicitly

## Must-Avoid
- QBRs that only show good news — customers don't trust them
- Vanity metrics (logins without context)
- Product roadmap slides with no connection to their stated needs
- Upsell pitches that come before demonstrating current product value
- Leaving without agreed next steps and owners

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- System adoption rate: % of eligible users active
- Primary success metric vs. baseline (e.g. support tickets per week)
- Usage trend: month-over-month improvement
- ARR and contract end date
- Upsell: additional module cost and expected benefit
- Health score (if tracked internally)

---

## Example Outline (HR Technology — Manufacturing Client)

1. Workforce Pro × [Client Name] — Q1 2025 Business Review
2. Agenda: Performance, Roadmap, Next Quarter
3. Our Shared Goals: 80% Adoption, 50% Reduction in Support Tickets
4. Q1 Performance: Adoption 67% (Target 80%), Support Tickets 3/Week (was 12)
5. Key Wins: Scheduling Time –40%, Compliance Flags Resolved
6. Usage Deep Dive: 2,800 Employees, Active Teams, Feature Utilization
7. Where We Fell Short: Adoption Gap in Shift-Worker Tier — Root Cause + Fix
8. What's Coming: Compliance Module, Shift Trading Feature (Q3)
9. Our Recommendations: Push Mobile Adoption, Train Team Leads
10. Expansion Opportunity: Compliance Module — $28K, Solves Your Q3 Risk Item
11. Renewal: Contract Expires [Date] — Proposed Terms
12. Action Items: Yours + Ours, Owners + Dates
