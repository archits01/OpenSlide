# Content Strategy Presentation

**What it is:** A structured plan for how a company will use content to achieve specific marketing objectives — covering content pillars, formats, channels, production plan, and measurement.

---

## Audience
VP Marketing, CMO, or marketing leadership. Audience wants to see strategic logic (why this content, not just what content) and a realistic production plan. They're often approving budget and headcount.

## Tone
Strategic and analytical. Content strategy presentations should demonstrate that content decisions are based on customer behavior and business outcomes — not "we should post more on LinkedIn."

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Title** — Company name, period (e.g. "6-Month Content Strategy: Q3–Q4 2025")
2. **Current State Audit** — What content exists, current traffic/engagement, what's converting and what isn't
3. **The Problem** — Why the current approach isn't working. Data-backed diagnosis.
4. **Content Objectives** — What we want content to do: awareness, lead generation, activation, retention. Each with a target metric.
5. **Our Buyer Journey** — Awareness → consideration → decision. What questions buyers have at each stage.
6. **Content Pillars** — 3 strategic topics. Each with a rationale tied to buyer questions and SEO opportunity.
7. **Content Formats + Channels** — What we'll produce (long-form, video, tools, case studies) and where it lives.
8. **Distribution Plan** — Organic, paid amplification, email, partnerships. Not just "publish and pray."
9. **Production Plan** — Volume per month, format breakdown, internal vs. external resource requirements.
10. **Budget** — Total investment, broken down by creation, distribution, tools.
11. **Measurement Framework** — Leading indicators (traffic, engagement) and lagging outcomes (leads, pipeline).
12. **Timeline + Milestones** — Content calendar structure and key launch dates.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Current state audit with data — not just "we don't have enough content"
- A buyer journey map showing content needs at each stage
- Content pillars with SEO/demand rationale, not just topic lists
- Distribution plan — creation without distribution is wasted effort
- Production volume and resource requirements (who makes it)
- Budget broken out by creation vs. distribution vs. tools

## Must-Avoid
- Content strategies built around formats rather than buyer needs
- Pillar choices based on what's easiest to write, not what buyers search for
- Plans that don't address distribution — publication is not amplification
- Measurement frameworks that only measure vanity metrics (impressions, followers)
- Production plans with no resource allocation — just a wishlist of content types

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Current organic traffic volume (visits/month)
- Current content-attributed leads (per month or quarter)
- Target: traffic, MQLs, pipeline attributed to content
- Content production volume: pieces per month by type
- Budget: total and split by creation / distribution / tools
- Current conversion rate from blog visitor → lead (if trackable)

---

## Example Outline (B2B SaaS — Sustainability Reporting)

1. Content Strategy H2 2025: From Awareness to Pipeline
2. Current State: 12,000 Visits/Month, 3 Leads/Month — What's Wrong
3. Diagnosis: We Have Awareness Content, No Conversion Content
4. Content Objectives: Double MQLs to 25/Month in 6 Months
5. Our Buyer Journey: What Compliance Officers Ask Before Buying
6. Three Content Pillars: Regulatory Guides, ROI Tools, Customer Case Studies
7. Formats + Channels: Long-Form, Calculators, Video Case Studies, LinkedIn
8. Distribution: SEO + Paid Amplification + Email Nurture
9. Production Plan: 8 Pieces/Month — In-House vs. Freelance
10. Budget: £85K — Creation £52K, Distribution £24K, Tools £9K
11. Measurement: Traffic (lag), Lead Quality Score (leading), Pipeline Attributed (lag)
12. Timeline: Launch Phase 1 by August 1
