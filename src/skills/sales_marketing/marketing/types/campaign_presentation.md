# Campaign Presentation

**What it is:** A structured presentation of a marketing campaign — covering the strategic brief, creative concept, channel plan, budget allocation, and measurement framework. Used to gain internal approval or brief a team.

---

## Audience
CMO, VP Marketing, Regional VPs, or agency leadership. Evaluating creative concept, strategic logic, and execution confidence. They want to feel the campaign before seeing the media plan.

## Tone
Bold and confident. Campaign presentations should feel like the campaign — bring the creative energy into the room. Back it with data, but lead with the idea.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Campaign name, product/product line, time period
2. **The Brief** — Business objective, target segment, key insight driving the campaign
3. **The Audience** — Who we're talking to. Not just demographics — mindset, moment, motivation.
4. **The Insight** — The single truth about the audience or category that the campaign is built on
5. **The Idea** — Campaign concept in one sentence. The organizing creative idea.
6. **Creative Execution** — How the idea comes to life: headline, visuals, story. Show the work.
7. **Campaign Architecture** — How it all fits: hero content, amplification, always-on. The full system.
8. **Channel Plan** — Where it runs: TV, digital, OOH, social, email. Each with rationale and format.
9. **Budget Breakdown** — Total budget, allocation by channel, production vs. media split.
10. **Measurement Framework** — Primary KPI, secondary metrics, attribution methodology.
11. **Timeline** — Pre-launch, launch, optimization, and wrap phases.
12. **What Success Looks Like** — The target: new accounts, reach, uplift, awareness shift.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- A single campaign insight — not a list of facts about the audience
- Creative execution shown, not described — at minimum a headline and visual direction
- Channel plan with rationale for each channel choice
- Budget breakdown: total and split by channel/production
- Primary KPI with target number (not just "increase awareness")
- Comparison to previous campaign results if available

## Must-Avoid
- Campaign briefs masquerading as campaign presentations — show the creative
- Channel plan that lists every channel without rationale for inclusion
- Budget slides showing only the total — audiences want the breakdown
- Measurement frameworks with 8+ KPIs — pick the one that matters
- Skipping the "why this insight" step — insight is the most important slide

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Campaign budget: total and per-channel
- Previous campaign benchmark (opens, conversions, reach)
- Target: primary metric with number (e.g. "45,000 new savings account opens")
- Target audience size / reach estimate
- Campaign duration
- Media split: broadcast vs. digital vs. OOH %

---

## Example Outline (Bank Q3 Savings Campaign)

1. "Your Future, Secured" — Q3 Savings Account Campaign
2. The Brief: 45,000 New Savings Account Opens in 90 Days
3. Our Target: 35–54, Financially Anxious, Actively Considering a Move
4. The Insight: People Don't Open Savings Accounts — They Make Promises to Their Future Self
5. The Idea: "Your Future, Secured" — Savings as Self-Commitment
6. Creative Execution: TV Spot, OOH, Digital Variants
7. Campaign Architecture: Hero → Amplify → Convert
8. Channel Plan: TV $720K, Digital $840K, OOH $240K
9. Budget: $1.8M — Production + Media Breakdown
10. Measurement: Primary KPI = New Account Opens. Secondary = Awareness Lift.
11. Timeline: Pre-Launch July, Launch Aug 1, Optimization Aug–Sep
12. What Success Looks Like vs. Q2 (31,000 opens)
