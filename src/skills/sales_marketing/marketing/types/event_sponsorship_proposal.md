# Event Sponsorship Proposal

**What it is:** A presentation proposing investment in a specific event sponsorship — making the business case for the spend based on audience reach, ICP alignment, pipeline potential, and ROI model.

---

## Audience
CEO, CFO, VP Marketing, or budget committee. They want to see a credible ROI model, not just the reach statistics the event organizer gave you. Show the math behind the decision.

## Tone
Commercial and rigorous. Build the case from first principles — ICP attendance, past pipeline from similar events, break-even analysis. Don't rely on "brand visibility" as the primary justification.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Title** — Event name, sponsorship tier, investment amount, decision date
2. **Why This Event** — Event context, why it's relevant to our ICP, this year's attendance profile
3. **Our Target Audience at This Event** — ICP match: titles, company sizes, industries attending. Named % of attendees that fit.
4. **What We Get** — Sponsorship benefits: tier, speaking slot, booth, branding, pre/post event access.
5. **Previous Event Performance** — Pipeline from similar events we've attended. Sets the benchmark.
6. **The ROI Model** — Conversations expected → qualified leads → opportunities → closed deals. Show the math.
7. **Break-Even Analysis** — At our ACV, how many closed deals does this require to break even?
8. **Activation Plan** — What we'll do at the event. Booth strategy, session topic, pre-event outreach, post-event follow-up.
9. **Competitive Context** — Are our key competitors sponsoring? At what tier? What does our absence or presence signal?
10. **What We'd Pass Up** — What other investments this budget could fund. Honest trade-off framing.
11. **Recommendation + Ask** — Sponsor at [tier], invest $[X], with [activation plan]. Decision needed by [date].

---

## Must-Have Elements
- ICP match analysis for this specific event — not just total attendance
- Historical pipeline data from similar event sponsorships
- A break-even calculation based on ACV and expected win rate
- Activation plan — what we'll actually do there to convert spend into pipeline
- Competitive intelligence — are competitors sponsoring
- A clear recommendation, not just options

## Must-Avoid
- Leading with the event's prestige without connecting to your ICP
- ROI models that assume unrealistic conversion rates
- Proposals that treat brand awareness as a measurable outcome without a proxy metric
- Forgetting to include activation costs (booth design, travel, pre-event outreach) in the total investment
- Presenting without historical data — even rough estimates from past events

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Total investment: sponsorship fee + activation costs + travel
- Event total attendance and ICP-matching attendance estimate
- Expected conversations at event (booth + speaking + meetings)
- Past event pipeline: $ generated from similar investment
- Break-even: # closed deals needed at ACV
- Sales cycle: weeks from event meeting to closed-won

---

## Example Outline (Cybersecurity Startup — Black Hat USA)

1. Black Hat USA Sponsorship: Platinum Tier — $180K Investment
2. Why Black Hat: Our ICP Is There in Concentration
3. Target Audience: 3,400 Security Decision-Makers Matching Our ICP
4. What Platinum Gets Us: Speaking Slot + Booth + Pre-Event Access
5. What We've Seen Before: $2.8M Pipeline from Last 2 Security Events
6. The ROI Model: 400 Conversations → 40 Qualified → 12 Opportunities → 4 Closed
7. Break-Even: 4 Deals at $45K ACV = $180K — Payback in Single Event
8. Activation Plan: Executive Briefing Room + Workshop + Post-Event Sequence
9. Competitive Context: 3 Competitors Sponsoring — Absence Signals Weakness
10. The Trade-Off: What $180K Buys Us Otherwise
11. Recommendation: Sponsor Platinum, Decision Needed by April 30
