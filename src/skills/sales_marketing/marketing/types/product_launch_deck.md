# Product Launch Deck

**What it is:** An internal go-to-launch plan presentation — aligning GTM leadership on launch strategy, launch moment, channel plan, enablement requirements, and targets for a new product or major feature release.

---

## Audience
CEO, CMO, VP Sales, VP Product. Cross-functional exec alignment session before a major launch. Audience needs to be aligned on what's launching, when, to whom, and what each team owns.

## Tone
Action-oriented and specific. Launch presentations should create urgency and clarity — what's happening on launch day, who owns what, what success looks like in 90 days.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Product/feature name, launch date, presenter
2. **What We're Launching** — Product overview in 3 sentences. What it does, who it's for, what problem it solves.
3. **Why Now** — Market timing, customer demand signal, competitive context, strategic importance.
4. **Target Audience for Launch** — Primary segment for launch. Existing customers, new buyers, or both. ICP for new logos.
5. **Launch Targets** — Primary metric: adoption, revenue, or leads. With number and time horizon.
6. **Launch Tiers + Segments** — Phased rollout plan: beta, limited, general availability. Who gets access when.
7. **GTM Channels + Activities** — Email campaigns, events, press, partner activation, paid. Each with owner and launch date.
8. **Sales Enablement** — What sales needs: updated pitch, objection handling, demo script, competitive positioning.
9. **Pricing + Packaging** — How the new product is priced. Add-on, upgrade, new SKU. Available from [date].
10. **Launch Day Plan** — What happens on launch day: embargo lift, announcement, emails, social, press.
11. **Success Metrics + Tracking** — How adoption, revenue, and engagement are tracked. Dashboard owner.
12. **Owner Matrix** — Every workstream with a named owner and deadline. No ambiguity.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Launch date — explicit and agreed
- Primary success metric with target (e.g. "40% adoption by existing customers in 90 days")
- Named owners for each workstream
- Sales enablement plan — not just "we'll do a training"
- Launch day timeline (hour-by-hour or milestone-by-milestone)
- A clear definition of "existing customer vs. new logo" targeting

## Must-Avoid
- Launch decks that describe the product in depth without the GTM plan
- Success metrics without a tracking method
- Owner matrix at the end that's the first time ownership is assigned (should be pre-agreed)
- Launches with no consideration of the existing customer base
- Pricing not confirmed as part of the launch deck

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Launch date
- Feature/product adoption target: % in X days
- Revenue target: expansion ARR or new ARR from launch
- Existing customer base size (for adoption math)
- Email list size for launch announcement
- Sales team size (for enablement planning)

---

## Example Outline (Project Management SaaS — AI Features Launch)

1. Cascade AI: GTM Launch Plan — Launch Date [X]
2. What We're Launching: AI Task Prioritization, Automated Status Reports, Predictive Deadlines
3. Why Now: 3 Top-Requested Features + Competitive Timing
4. Target: Existing Enterprise Customers First, New Logos via Self-Serve
5. Launch Targets: 40% Adoption in 90 Days, 25% Increase in Expansion ARR
6. Rollout: Beta (Week 1–2) → GA for Enterprise (Week 3) → All Plans (Week 6)
7. GTM Channels: In-App, Email, Event, PR, Partner Activation
8. Sales Enablement: Demo Script + Objection Handling + Competitive Card
9. Pricing: AI Add-On — $15/seat/month for Enterprise, Included in Pro+ at Renewal
10. Launch Day: T–7 Embargo, T–0 Email + In-App + Press
11. Tracking: Adoption Dashboard (Product) + Revenue Tracking (Sales Ops)
12. Launch Owner Matrix: Every Workstream, Owner, and Deadline
