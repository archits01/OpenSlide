# Marketing Strategy Presentation

**What it is:** An annual or multi-year marketing plan presented to CEO, board, or exec leadership — covering strategic shift, channel mix, budget request, team structure, and targets.

---

## Audience
CEO, board, CFO, and exec team. Audience is approving a significant budget increase and a strategic shift. They want to see logical evidence for the bet, not just marketing enthusiasm.

## Tone
Strategic, commercially anchored, evidence-based. Don't just say "we're shifting to inbound" — show the economics of why that shift is correct. Connect every investment to revenue impact.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Title** — Company, period (e.g. "2025 Marketing Strategy"), presenter
2. **Where We Are Today** — Current funnel metrics: traffic, leads, MQLs, win rate, CAC, LTV. Honest baseline.
3. **What's Working and What Isn't** — Channels performing vs. underperforming. Lead quality, CAC by source.
4. **The Strategic Shift** — What we're changing in our marketing motion and why. Supported by data.
5. **Our Target for [Year]** — Revenue, customer, and pipeline targets. Named with numbers.
6. **Channel Strategy** — How we'll reach buyers: inbound, outbound, PLG, partnerships, events. Rationale per channel.
7. **Campaign Plan** — Key campaigns for the year, each with objective and target metric.
8. **Budget Request** — Total ask, breakdown by channel/team, comparison to last year.
9. **Team + Org** — Current team, proposed additions, skills required to execute.
10. **Key Metrics Dashboard** — The 5 metrics we'll track weekly. What good looks like.
11. **Risks + Dependencies** — What could prevent us from hitting targets. What we need from other teams.
12. **Year-End Target** — The number we're committing to. Repeat it clearly.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Current CAC and LTV — shows marketing understands unit economics
- Strategic rationale for the shift (why are we moving from outbound to inbound, or vice versa)
- Channel strategy with ROI rationale per channel, not just coverage
- Budget tied to outputs — "X budget generates Y MQLs at Z CAC"
- Headcount request with specific roles and rationale
- A single primary metric that measures success

## Must-Avoid
- Marketing strategies that are just a list of tactics without strategic logic
- Budget requests without CAC/pipeline models behind them
- Channel strategies based on "best practice" rather than company-specific data
- Omitting CAC trend — if it's rising, address it head-on
- Strategies that don't acknowledge current performance gaps

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Current ARR and growth rate
- CAC by channel (current vs. target)
- LTV:CAC ratio
- Pipeline coverage ratio
- Budget: total ask and % increase from prior year
- Target: customers, ARR, and pipeline by year-end

---

## Example Outline (B2B SaaS — Inbound-Led Shift)

1. Fieldwork 2025 Marketing Strategy
2. Where We Are: 180 Customers, $6.8M ARR, CAC $12.4K
3. What's Working (Partner Referrals) and What Isn't (Cold Outbound)
4. The Shift: From Outbound-Heavy to Inbound-Led + PLG
5. 2025 Targets: 280 Customers, $11M ARR
6. Channel Strategy: SEO/Content, PLG, Partner Channel, Demand Gen
7. Campaign Calendar: 4 Major Campaigns + ABM Program
8. Budget Request: $3.2M (40% Increase) — Channel Breakdown
9. Team: +3 Hires (Content, Demand Gen, Partner Manager)
10. Our North Star Metrics: CAC, Pipeline Coverage, MQL-to-Win
11. What Could Go Wrong + Dependencies on Sales
12. The Number We're Committing To: 280 Customers by December 31
