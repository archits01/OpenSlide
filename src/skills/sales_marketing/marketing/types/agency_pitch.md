# Agency Pitch

**What it is:** A competitive pitch deck used by a creative or digital agency to win a specific client brief — presenting strategic thinking, creative approach, team credentials, process, and commercial terms.

---

## Audience
Client-side decision makers: Marketing Director, CMO, Brand Director, procurement lead, and often a C-suite sponsor. Evaluating multiple agencies simultaneously. They want confidence, chemistry, and proof you understand their problem.

## Tone
Confident without arrogance. Strategic first, creative second. Specific — no agency clichés ("we tell stories," "we put people first"). Show you've done your homework on their business, competitors, and audience.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Agency name, client name, project name, date.
2. **The Brief, In Our Words** — Restate the client's problem in your own language. Shows listening. Surfaces any reframing.
3. **What's Really at Stake** — Elevate the brief: market context, competitive pressure, what failure costs them.
4. **Our Strategic Lens** — The core strategic idea or framework guiding the creative work. One sentence, defended.
5. **Creative Direction** — Visual and tonal direction. Mood references, early concept territory. Direction, not finished work.
6. **How We'd Approach This** — Process walkthrough: phases, milestones, decision points. How you work, not just what you make.
7. **The Team** — Named individuals who will work on this account. Roles, relevant experience, faces.
8. **Relevant Work** — 2–3 case studies chosen for relevance. Problem → approach → outcome (with metrics).
9. **Why Us** — Honest differentiator. What you have that other shortlisted agencies don't. One strong point.
10. **Timeline** — High-level phased plan from kick-off to delivery.
11. **Investment** — Budget breakdown by phase or workstream. Clear on inclusions and exclusions.
12. **Next Steps** — What happens after this meeting. Decision date, chemistry questions, availability.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Named team members with photos — not generic "our team" slide
- At minimum 2 case studies with outcome metrics (revenue, engagement, brand lift)
- A clear restatement of the brief — proves you listened
- One genuine differentiator, not a list of agency values
- Timeline granular enough to show you understand the scope
- Budget that maps to deliverables, not a single lump sum

## Must-Avoid
- Generic credentials slides with logo walls and headcount
- Creative work irrelevant to the client's category or problem
- Overpromising outcomes ("we'll transform your brand")
- Process slides describing generic waterfall project management
- Team slide showing partners who won't touch the account
- Closing with "any questions?" as the final slide

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Client's market position or relevant performance context (shows research)
- Case study results: revenue impact, conversion uplift, engagement rates, brand metrics
- Relevant credentials: years in category, similar-scale projects handled
- Proposed timeline: total project duration, phases, key milestones
- Budget: total, breakdown by phase/workstream
- Team: number of dedicated FTEs on this account
