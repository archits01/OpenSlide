# Media Kit

**What it is:** A sales and reference document for potential advertisers, sponsors, and partners — presenting audience profile, format options, pricing, and social proof from current partners.

---

## Audience
Advertising directors, media buyers, brand partnership managers, and CMOs evaluating sponsorship opportunities. Audience is comparing multiple publisher/media options. They need to quickly assess fit and value.

## Tone
Professional, data-rich, credible. Let the audience numbers do the selling. Use third-party data and engagement metrics where available — not just self-reported reach. Be precise, not hyperbolic.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Publication/platform name, tagline, media kit version/year
2. **Who We Are** — Brief overview: what you publish, how long you've been doing it, editorial focus
3. **Audience Overview** — Total reach, key demographic profile, seniority mix, geography
4. **Audience in Depth** — Job function breakdown, company size of readers, industries represented. Include third-party validation if available.
5. **Engagement Metrics** — Open rates, click rates, time on site, session frequency. Benchmarked against industry averages.
6. **Why Our Audience Matters** — The buying power, decision-making role, or influence of your readers. Make it relevant to the advertiser's ICP.
7. **Format Options** — Each available format with specs, example, and placement context (newsletter, events, reports, etc.)
8. **Pricing** — CPM, flat rates, or package pricing per format. Be transparent.
9. **Partnership Tiers** — If you offer bundled packages: what's included in each tier.
10. **What Partners Say** — 2–3 quotes or case studies from current advertisers with measurable outcomes.
11. **How to Get Started** — Contact info, booking process, lead time requirements.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Audience size with source and measurement methodology
- Engagement rates with industry benchmark comparison
- Job title/seniority breakdown — the most important metric for B2B advertisers
- At least one advertiser testimonial with a result metric
- Pricing per format — advertiser will ask for it
- Format specifications and visual examples

## Must-Avoid
- Audience numbers without methodology (how is "340,000 readers" defined?)
- Vanity metrics without context (followers without engagement rate)
- Pricing that requires a call to discuss — it signals lack of confidence
- Testimonials with no outcome metrics
- Formats listed without specs or visual examples

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Total monthly readers/subscribers
- Newsletter subscriber count and open rate vs. benchmark
- % senior leaders / C-suite in audience
- Email CPM or newsletter sponsorship rate
- Click-through rate on sponsored content
- Geography: % UK, US, international

1. The Stack — Media Kit 2025

11. Book a Conversation: Contact + Next Steps
