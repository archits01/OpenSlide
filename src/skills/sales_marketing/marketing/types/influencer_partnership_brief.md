# Influencer Partnership Brief

**What it is:** A structured brief presented to an influencer agency or marketing team, defining campaign objectives, creator selection criteria, content guidelines, budget, and success metrics.

---

## Audience
Influencer marketing agency, internal brand partnerships team, or creator management platform. Audience needs enough clarity to make creator recommendations and present a campaign plan without constant back-and-forth.

## Tone
Brand-aware and precise. Be specific about what the brand stands for (and doesn't). The brief should give creators creative freedom within a defined guardrail — not a script, but a clear brand voice.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Brand name, campaign name, quarter/period, brief issued by
2. **Brand Overview** — Who we are, what we sell, what makes us different. Brief and factual.
3. **Campaign Objective** — Primary goal: reach, conversion, trial, or repositioning. One objective with a target metric.
4. **Target Audience** — Demographics, psychographics, platform usage. Be specific — not "18–35 health-conscious consumers."
5. **Creator Profile** — Tier (macro/micro/nano), category (health, fitness, lifestyle), engagement rate minimum, audience demographic requirements.
6. **Content Guidelines** — Must-include: product, CTA, hashtag. Must-avoid: tone, topics, competitors, brand safety rules.
7. **Platform + Format** — Where content lives: Instagram, TikTok, YouTube. Which formats: Reels, Story, Long-form.
8. **Budget + Partnership Count** — Total budget, number of creators by tier, fee range per creator, usage rights.
9. **Timeline** — Brief → selection → briefing → content creation → review → publish.
10. **Measurement + Reporting** — How success is tracked: tracking links, UTM codes, promo codes. Who reports what and when.
11. **Brand Safety Rules** — Clear list of topics, language, accounts, and categories the creators must not associate with.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Specific creator tier and category criteria — not just "relevant influencers"
- Brand voice examples: "we sound like X, not Y"
- Content must-haves and must-avoids — creative freedom within clear guardrails
- Budget with breakdown by tier (macro vs. micro)
- Tracking methodology: how conversions and reach are measured
- Brand safety rules explicitly stated

## Must-Avoid
- Briefs that are so prescriptive creators can't be authentic
- No clear conversion tracking plan — "brand awareness only" is a cop-out
- Vague audience descriptions
- Leaving brand safety requirements implicit — write them out
- Campaign briefs with no example content or reference creators

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Total campaign budget (£/$) and split by tier
- Number of creators: macro (X), micro (Y)
- Minimum engagement rate requirement
- Target reach
- Target conversion metric (tracked conversions, promo code redemptions)
- Timeline: campaign start/end dates

---

## Example Outline (UK Nutrition Brand — Q1 Campaign)

1. Zest × [Agency Name]: Q1 Influencer Campaign Brief
2. Zest: Who We Are and What We Stand For
3. Campaign Objective: 2,400 Tracked Conversions in 90 Days
4. Our Target Audience: UK Women 25–38, Active Lifestyle, Wellness-First
5. Creator Profile: 15 Creators — 3 Macro (500K+), 12 Micro (50–200K), Health/Wellness/Fitness
6. What Creators Must Include and Avoid
7. Platforms + Formats: Instagram Reels, Stories; TikTok
8. Budget: £120K — Macro £60K, Micro £45K, Production £15K
9. Timeline: Creator Selection (W1) → Content Review (W3) → Publish (W5)
10. Measurement: Unique Promo Codes + UTM Links + Dashboard Reporting Weekly
11. Brand Safety Rules: No Alcohol, No Competitor Content, No Medical Claims
