# Brand Presentation

**What it is:** An internal or external presentation introducing a new or refreshed brand identity — covering positioning, visual system, tone of voice, and how to bring it to life.

---

## Audience
Internal: full company, leadership, retail partners, or cross-functional stakeholders. Audience needs to understand the why before the what — otherwise the new logo is just a logo.

## Tone
Storytelling-led, emotionally resonant, clear. Brand presentations that lead with rationale before revealing visuals create emotional buy-in. Avoid design jargon. Make everyone feel like the brand belongs to them.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Brand name, presentation title (e.g. "Introducing Our New Brand Identity")
2. **Why Now: The Brand Journey** — Company history, inflection point, why the brand needed to evolve
3. **Where We Were** — Old brand positioning: what it stood for, what it communicated, where it fell short
4. **What We Heard** — Customer research, employee insights, partner feedback that shaped the new direction
5. **Our New Positioning** — Brand promise in one sentence. What we stand for and for whom.
6. **Brand Personality** — 3–4 adjectives with examples. "We are X, not Y" format works best.
7. **Tone of Voice** — How we write and speak. Examples of before/after copy.
8. **Visual Identity: Logo** — The new logo, what it means, what it replaced and why.
9. **Visual Identity: Color + Typography** — Palette rationale, primary typefaces, usage rules.
10. **In Practice: Brand Applications** — Real examples: website, packaging, ads, social, signage.
11. **What This Means for You** — Team-specific guidance. Sales: how to talk about the brand. Marketing: where to find assets. All-hands: the simple version.
12. **The Brand Launch Plan** — Timeline, channels, milestones, external announcement date.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- A "why now" narrative — not just a reveal
- Customer or research evidence behind the positioning change
- Brand personality with positive and negative examples ("we are X, not Y")
- Real applications, not just isolated design elements
- Team-specific guidance on how to use the new brand
- Clear launch timeline with external announcement plan

## Must-Avoid
- Opening with the new logo before earning the emotional setup
- Design slides with no rationale — "we chose blue because it's our color"
- Brand presentations that talk only to marketers — this is for everyone
- Tone of voice slides with no examples
- Showing applications that haven't been approved or fully built yet
- Ending without telling people where to access brand assets

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Years since last brand refresh
- Research inputs: number of customer interviews, survey respondents
- New positioning statement (quoted exactly)
- Brand application timeline: first external use date
- Asset access location (brand portal, Google Drive, etc.)
- Company context: employees, years in business, revenue milestone that triggered the change

---

## Example Outline (DTC Furniture Company Rebrand)

1. Arlo — A New Chapter
2. Nine Years In: How We Got Here
3. Where We Started: Catalogue, Product-First, Traditional
4. What Our Customers Told Us They Needed
5. Our New Positioning: "Furniture That Earns Its Place"
6. Our Brand Personality: Considered, Warm, Honest, Enduring
7. How We Sound: Voice + Tone
8. The New Visual Identity: Logo
9. Color, Typography, Photography Direction
10. Arlo In Practice: Digital, Retail, Campaign
11. What This Means for Your Team
12. Launch Plan: What Happens Next
