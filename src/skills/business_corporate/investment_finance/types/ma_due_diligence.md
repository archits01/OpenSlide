# M&A / Due Diligence Presentation

**What it is:** A structured overview presentation prepared for a potential acquirer, investment committee, or board — summarizing the target company's business, financials, strategic fit, and key diligence findings.

---

## Audience
Investment committee, CEO/CFO, board M&A committee, or integration planning team. Audience is making or approving a significant capital allocation decision. They want clarity on what they're buying and what risks they're taking on.

## Tone
Objective, precise, and deal-oriented. M&A presentations should present findings without spin — both the value drivers and the risks. Surprises in diligence kill deals; surprises post-close kill careers.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Target company name, transaction type, proposed deal terms, date
2. **Transaction Overview** — Deal structure, purchase price, consideration type, timeline
3. **Target Business Overview** — What the company does, market position, business model
4. **Strategic Rationale** — Why this acquisition. Synergy thesis: revenue, cost, market access.
5. **Market + Competitive Position** — Target's market, competitive differentiation, moat
6. **Financial Summary** — Revenue, growth rate, EBITDA/gross margin, ARR (if applicable), burn
7. **Historical Financial Performance** — 3 years of revenue, margin, and key metrics
8. **Revenue Quality** — Customer concentration, contract length, churn, NRR. Revenue durability.
9. **Key Risks** — Top diligence concerns: customer concentration, technical debt, key man, IP.
10. **Synergy Analysis** — Revenue synergies (specific, with assumptions) and cost synergies
11. **Integration Plan** — Key integration workstreams, timeline, resource requirements, quick wins
12. **Valuation** — Proposed valuation methodology, comps, DCF summary, price rationale
13. **Recommendation** — Proceed / conditions / pass. Board decision requested.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Strategic rationale: why this target, why now, what does it accomplish
- Revenue quality analysis — customer concentration and NRR are critical
- Named risks with materiality assessment
- Synergy analysis with assumptions stated — not just synergy headlines
- Valuation methodology with comps
- Integration plan (high-level) showing someone has thought about execution
- A recommendation, not just a presentation of findings

## Must-Avoid
- Due diligence presentations that list only upside without risks
- Revenue synergy projections without named assumptions
- Customer concentration buried in appendix — it belongs in the main deck
- Integration plans that are just a list of work to be done without timeline or owners
- Valuations without sensitivity analysis

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Purchase price and enterprise value
- Revenue and growth rate (3 years)
- EBITDA margin or gross margin
- Customer concentration: top 3–5 customers as % of revenue
- NRR / churn rate
- Synergy estimate: $ and timeline

---

## Example Outline (Strategic Acquisition — Software Company)

1. Project [Codename] — Acquisition Summary for Investment Committee
2. Transaction Overview: $[X]M All-Cash, Expected Close [Date]
3. [Target] Business Overview: What They Do and Where They Play
4. Strategic Rationale: Why [Target], Why Now
5. Market Position: $[X]B TAM, [Target]'s Competitive Moat
6. Financial Summary: $[X]M ARR, 42% YoY, 68% Gross Margin
7. Historical Financials: FY22–FY24 Performance
8. Revenue Quality: Top 5 Customer Concentration, NRR 108%
9. Key Diligence Risks: [3 Named Risks with Materiality]
10. Synergy Analysis: $[X]M Revenue + $[X]M Cost in 24 Months
11. Integration Plan: 5 Workstreams, 18-Month Plan
12. Valuation: [X]× ARR — Comps, DCF, Sensitivity
13. Recommendation: Proceed at [Price] Subject to [Conditions]
