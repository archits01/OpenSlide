# Earnings Presentation

**What it is:** A quarterly or annual financial results presentation for investors and analysts — reporting performance vs. guidance, explaining drivers, and providing forward guidance.

---

## Audience
Public market investors, equity analysts, institutional holders, and retail investors. Analysts will model the guidance you give. Every word in this presentation is parsed. Legal, IR, and finance teams all review before publication.

## Tone
Measured, precise, and consistent. Earnings presentations have legal implications — avoid hyperbole. Be consistent with the earnings release language. Use the same metrics and definitions every quarter.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, quarter/year, earnings call date
2. **Safe Harbor / Forward-Looking Statements** — Legal requirement. Standard language.
3. **Q[X] Highlights** — 5–7 bullet summary: revenue, key operating metric, strategic milestone.
4. **Financial Summary** — Revenue, non-GAAP gross profit, operating income, EPS. Vs. prior quarter and year.
5. **Revenue Breakdown** — By segment, geography, or product line. Growth rates per segment.
6. **Key Operating Metrics** — The company's disclosed KPIs: ARR, customers, NRR, bookings, etc.
7. **Gross Margin Analysis** — Margin drivers and trends. GAAP vs. non-GAAP reconciliation.
8. **Operating Expenses** — R&D, S&M, G&A. Trend and as % of revenue.
9. **Free Cash Flow + Balance Sheet** — FCF, cash position, debt.
10. **Q[X+1] Guidance** — Revenue, operating income, EPS guidance range. Confirm or revise annual guidance.
11. **Full Year Guidance** — Updated annual guidance with any changes from prior quarter.
12. **Strategic Highlights** — 2–3 non-financial achievements or milestones worth noting.
13. **Appendix** — GAAP to non-GAAP reconciliation, segment detail, customer count methodology.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Safe harbor statement (legally required for forward-looking statements)
- GAAP to non-GAAP reconciliation in the appendix
- Revenue vs. guidance and vs. consensus (if applicable)
- Forward guidance: specific range for next quarter and full year
- Consistent metric definitions — don't change how you define ARR mid-year
- Balance sheet and cash position

## Must-Avoid
- Metrics presented without year-over-year and sequential comparison
- Guidance given as a range wider than 2–3% — it signals internal uncertainty
- Non-GAAP metrics without clear GAAP reconciliation
- Changing metric definitions without explicit disclosure
- Burying misses — analysts track guidance accuracy across quarters

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Total revenue: GAAP and non-GAAP
- Gross margin: GAAP and non-GAAP
- Operating income/loss
- EPS: GAAP and non-GAAP
- Free cash flow
- Key operating metric (ARR, subscribers, GMV — whatever the company discloses)
- Q+1 and full-year guidance

1. Q2 2025 Earnings — [Company Name]
2. Forward-Looking Statements
3. Q2 Highlights
4. Q2 Financial Summary
5. Revenue: $[X]M (+32% YoY) — Segment Breakdown

7. Gross Margin: 74% non-GAAP
8. Operating Expenses

13. Appendix: GAAP Reconciliation
