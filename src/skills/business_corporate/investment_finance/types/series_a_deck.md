# Series A Investor Deck

**What it is:** A fundraising presentation for a Series A round — making the case for market opportunity, product differentiation, team strength, traction, and the investment thesis.

---

## Audience
Series A investors: partners at institutional VC firms. They evaluate hundreds of decks. They're looking for large market, differentiated product, early signs of product-market fit, and a capable team. They will probe every assumption.

## Tone
Confident and evidence-backed. Series A investors have seen thousands of decks — avoid clichés ("we're the Airbnb for X"). Show genuine traction, honest unit economics, and a defensible market view.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, tagline, founders
2. **The Problem** — The specific, painful problem you're solving. Market-level evidence of its scale.
3. **The Solution** — What you've built. How it works. Why it's meaningfully better.
4. **Market Size** — TAM, SAM, SOM with methodology. Be honest about what's serviceable.
5. **Product** — Key product screenshots or workflow. Show, don't just describe.
6. **Traction** — ARR, growth rate, logo count, key customers. Your strongest evidence of PMF.
7. **Business Model** — How you make money. ACV, pricing model, payback period.
8. **Unit Economics** — CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC ratio. Gross margin. Payback period trend.
9. **Go-to-Market** — How you acquire customers. What's working, what you're expanding.
10. **Team** — Founders + key hires. Why this team for this problem. Relevant track record.
11. **Competition** — Honest competitive landscape. Why you win.
12. **The Ask** — Amount raising, how you'll use it, what you'll achieve.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Traction slide with real ARR and growth rate — the most important slide at Series A
- Unit economics: LTV:CAC ratio and gross margin
- Market sizing with methodology, not just a large TAM number
- A clear investment thesis: why this company wins in this market
- Honest competition slide — VCs will ask
- Specific use of proceeds tied to milestones

## Must-Avoid
- TAM slides showing the entire market without a SOM estimate
- Traction slides with only user counts (without revenue or engagement)
- Team slides with titles but no relevant background
- Avoiding pricing — "pricing TBD" is a red flag at Series A
- Overloaded slides — each slide should make one point

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Current ARR and growth rate (MoM or YoY)
- NRR (net revenue retention)
- Gross margin %
- CAC and LTV (or LTV:CAC ratio)
- Customer count and logo names (if permitted)
- Round size and post-money valuation

---

## Example Outline (B2B SaaS Series A)

1. [Company] — [One-Line Value Proposition]
2. The Problem: [Specific, Painful, Large-Scale]
3. The Solution: [How It Works + Why Better]
4. Market: $2.1B SAM, $8.4B TAM, Growing 28% YoY
5. Product: Key Workflow [Screenshots / Demo Flow]
6. Traction: $2.4M ARR, 3× YoY, 87 Customers
7. Business Model: $28K ACV, Annual, 8-Month Payback
8. Unit Economics: LTV:CAC 4.8×, 72% Gross Margin
9. GTM: Inbound + Outbound, Expanding to Channel
10. Team: Founders + 4 Key Hires
11. Competition: Why We Win Against [A], [B], [C]
12. The Ask: $12M Series A — Hiring Plan + Milestones
