# Quarterly Business Review (Internal)

**What it is:** An internal quarterly business review presented to senior leadership — covering company-wide performance vs. plan across all functions, identifying issues, and aligning on priorities for the next quarter.

---

## Audience
CEO, CFO, and executive leadership team. Cross-functional — each department head presents their section. The QBR surfaces issues that monthly ops reviews might miss and sets the next quarter's priorities.

## Tone
Analytical and candid. QBRs are internal — there's no reason to spin. The value comes from honest diagnosis of what happened and why. Good QBRs are uncomfortable — they surface the real issues.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, quarter, date, facilitator
2. **Executive Summary** — Headline: did we hit plan? Key wins, key misses, 3 priorities for Q[X+1].
3. **Company Scorecard** — Top 10 metrics vs. plan. Red/Yellow/Green status.
4. **Revenue + ARR Performance** — Bookings, ARR, revenue. Vs. plan and prior quarter.
5. **Sales Performance** — Pipeline, quota attainment, win rates, top deals won/lost.
6. **Customer Success Performance** — Renewal rate, NRR, churn analysis, CSAT.
7. **Product + Engineering** — Shipped vs. roadmap. Velocity. Key misses and their cause.
8. **Marketing Performance** — Pipeline contribution, MQLs, CAC by channel.
9. **Finance Review** — Revenue, burn, gross margin, vs. plan. Runway update.
10. **People** — Headcount, attrition, open roles, time-to-hire. Culture/eNPS if tracked.
11. **Q[X+1] Priorities** — Top 3–5 company-level priorities for next quarter. Named owners.
12. **Decisions Needed** — Any cross-functional issues requiring exec alignment.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Company scorecard with Red/Yellow/Green status — immediately shows where problems are
- Actuals vs. plan for every major metric
- Root cause for any Red or significant Yellow
- Q+1 priorities with named owners
- Cross-functional issues requiring decisions surfaced explicitly
- Honest assessment from each function — not a highlight reel

## Must-Avoid
- QBRs that are status reports without analysis
- Department sections that only show wins
- Metrics with no plan comparison
- Too many metrics — 8–12 top-line metrics per function is enough
- Leaving without agreed Q+1 priorities and owners

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Revenue vs. plan %
- ARR vs. plan %
- Gross margin vs. plan
- Burn rate vs. plan
- Headcount vs. plan
- Pipeline coverage ratio

---

## Example Outline (Series B Company — Q2 QBR)

1. Q2 2025 Quarterly Business Review
2. Q2 Executive Summary: 91% of Plan, 3 Reds to Discuss
3. Company Scorecard: 12 Metrics, Red/Yellow/Green
4. Revenue + ARR: $2.1M Bookings (88% of Plan), $8.2M ARR

9. Finance: $1.9M Burn, 18-Month Runway, Gross Margin 72%
10. People: 96 FTEs, 3 Attrition, 7 Open Roles
11. Q3 Priorities: 5 Named Initiatives + Owners
12. Decisions: [Cross-Functional Issues for Exec Alignment]
