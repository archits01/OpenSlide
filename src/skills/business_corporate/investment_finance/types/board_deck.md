# Board Deck

**What it is:** A quarterly or monthly board of directors meeting presentation — covering company performance, key decisions required, strategic updates, and financial health.

---

## Audience
Board of directors: independent directors, investor-appointed board members, and observer seats. Audience has fiduciary responsibility. They want facts, not spin. They're comparing what management said last quarter to what actually happened.

## Tone
Direct, transparent, and structured. Boards respect candor — bad news delivered early with analysis is far better than bad news buried in slides. Use the same format every quarter for comparability.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, board meeting date, quarter, presenter
2. **Executive Summary** — 5-7 bullet highlights: what went well, what didn't, key decisions needed from board.
3. **Financial Performance** — Revenue, ARR/MRR, gross margin, burn, runway. Vs. plan and vs. prior quarter.
4. **Revenue Deep Dive** — ARR bridge: new, expansion, contraction, churn. Cohort analysis if material.
5. **Key Operating Metrics** — The 5–8 metrics this company's board tracks. Trend view, at-a-plan indicator.
6. **Sales + GTM Performance** — Pipeline, bookings, win rates, sales cycle, headcount vs. plan.
7. **Product + Engineering Update** — Shipped vs. roadmap. Key milestones. Headcount vs. plan.
8. **Headcount + People** — Total headcount, new hires, attrition, key open roles.
9. **Cash + Runway** — Cash balance, monthly burn rate, runway in months. Next capital event.
10. **Strategic Update** — 2–3 strategic items not covered elsewhere. Company-level decisions or pivots.
11. **Risks + Issues** — Named risks with owner and mitigation status. Not a comprehensive risk register.
12. **Decisions Required** — Explicit list of items requiring board approval or input.
13. **Appendix** — Detailed financials, department metrics, investor update appendix.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Actuals vs. plan — not just actuals in isolation
- ARR bridge showing new, expansion, and churn
- Cash runway in months — the most important number for early-stage boards
- Explicit list of decisions required from the board
- Consistent format quarter-over-quarter (comparability matters)
- At least one honest acknowledgment of a miss with root cause

## Must-Avoid
- Board decks that only show good news — credibility is the board's primary currency with management
- Metrics that change every quarter (makes trending impossible)
- Operating metrics buried in appendix — they belong in the main deck
- Decision items that come as a surprise — pre-read should be sent 48-72 hours in advance
- Excessive slide count — most board decks should be 12–18 slides plus appendix

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- ARR: total, growth rate, net revenue retention
- Gross margin %
- Monthly burn rate and runway (months)
- Pipeline: $ and coverage ratio
- Headcount: total, vs. plan
- Cash balance
