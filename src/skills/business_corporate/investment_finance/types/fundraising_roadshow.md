# Fundraising Roadshow Deck

**What it is:** A polished investor pitch used during a formal fundraising process — presented to multiple investors over a short period, balancing completeness with repeatability.

---

## Audience
Multiple investor types: institutional VCs (Partners, Principals), growth equity firms, strategic investors, and family offices. Deck is sent as leave-behind and presented in 30–45 minute meetings.

## Tone
Authoritative and consistent. Roadshow decks are presented 20–40 times — they need to be crisp enough to pitch in 25 minutes and dense enough to answer first-round diligence questions. Every claim must withstand repeated scrutiny.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, round size, tagline
2. **Company Snapshot** — ARR, growth, customers, team size, founded. One page.
3. **The Problem** — Market-validated pain. Specific, with evidence of scale.
4. **The Solution + Product** — What you built, how it works, why it's hard to replicate.
5. **Market Size** — TAM, SAM, SOM. Bottoms-up methodology.
6. **Traction** — ARR bridge, growth rate, customer logos, NRR, key cohort data.
7. **Unit Economics** — LTV:CAC, gross margin, payback period, burn multiple.
8. **Business Model** — Pricing, ACV, expansion mechanics, contract structure.
9. **GTM + Sales Motion** — How you acquire customers, channel mix, productivity metrics.
10. **Competitive Positioning** — Landscape overview, why you win.
11. **Team** — Founders and key leaders. Why this team.
12. **Financial Plan + Milestones** — 3-year model summary, what you're raising, use of proceeds.
13. **The Ask** — Round size, target close date, lead investor preference.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- ARR and ARR growth rate on slide 2 — investors orient on these immediately
- Traction cohort data or NRR — proves retention quality
- Unit economics: burn multiple, LTV:CAC, gross margin
- Specific use of proceeds tied to named milestones
- Credible 3-year financial plan (not just targets — show the model logic)
- A clear lead investor preference or process timeline

## Must-Avoid
- Roadshow decks over 15 slides (presentations should leave room for Q&A)
- Vague market sizing without methodology
- Financial projections that hockey-stick without operational explanation
- Avoiding burn multiple — investors calculate it themselves if you don't show it
- Inconsistent metrics across slides (define and stick to your ARR definition)

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- ARR: current and trailing 12-month growth %
- NRR (net revenue retention)
- Gross margin %
- LTV:CAC ratio
- Burn multiple (net new ARR / net burn)
- Round size and post-money valuation range
