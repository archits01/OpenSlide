# Analyst / Investor Day Presentation

**What it is:** A multi-hour investor and analyst event presentation — making the case for long-term company strategy, financial model, and market leadership for buy-side and sell-side audiences.

---

## Audience
Buy-side analysts, portfolio managers, sell-side equity analysts, institutional investors, and long-term holders. Sophisticated financial audience. They're building or updating their financial models.

## Tone
Strategic, evidence-based, and forward-looking. Analyst day is a rare opportunity to set the long-term narrative. Be aspirational but credible — every long-term target must be defensible.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, investor day date, CEO and CFO names
2. **Company Overview** — Where we are today: market position, scale, key metrics.
3. **The Market Opportunity** — Why this market, why now, how large the opportunity is.
4. **Our Strategy** — 3–5 strategic pillars for the next 3–5 years.
5. **Competitive Moat** — What makes the company's position durable. Network effects, switching costs, data advantages.
6. **Product Vision** — Where the product is going. Long-horizon roadmap.
7. **GTM + Growth Engine** — How we scale: land + expand, channel, international.
8. **Unit Economics + Business Model** — LTV:CAC, gross margin trajectory, scaling dynamics.
9. **Financial Model** — 3–5 year revenue, margin, and FCF targets with drivers.
10. **Capital Allocation** — R&D investment, M&A framework, buyback / return of capital (public companies).
11. **Customer Evidence** — Named enterprise customers with outcomes and expansion stories.
12. **Management Team** — Leadership depth and succession.
13. **Financial Targets Summary** — The key numbers being committed to. Clear and unambiguous.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Long-term financial targets with stated assumptions
- Gross margin expansion story and timeline
- Rule of 40 trajectory (or equivalent efficiency metric for stage)
- Competitive moat analysis — why the position is defensible
- Capital allocation framework
- Management credibility: past performance and track record

## Must-Avoid
- Analyst day targets set so conservatively they're not worth setting
- Strategies that can't be explained in 3 bullet points
- Financial models with hockey-stick assumptions but no operational explanation
- Avoiding difficult questions about competition or market headwinds
- Missing the FCF/profitability trajectory — long-term investors care about FCF more than revenue growth alone

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- 3–5 year revenue targets and growth rates
- Long-term gross margin target
- Long-term operating margin or FCF margin target
- Total addressable market (quantified)
- NRR and expansion revenue as % of total
- Rule of 40 score (current and target)
