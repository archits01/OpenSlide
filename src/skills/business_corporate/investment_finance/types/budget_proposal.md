# Budget Proposal / Annual Plan

**What it is:** A presentation of the proposed annual operating budget — covering revenue targets, cost plans, investment priorities, headcount, and the strategic rationale for resource allocation decisions.

---

## Audience
CFO, CEO, board of directors, and department heads. Audience is approving the full-year financial plan. CFO wants to see tight assumptions. CEO wants to see strategic alignment. Board wants to see path to financial health.

## Tone
Rigorous and strategic. Budget presentations should demonstrate that every dollar is allocated to a specific outcome, not just "competitive with last year." Show the thinking behind the allocation, not just the numbers.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, fiscal year, presenter
2. **Planning Context** — Strategic priorities that drive this budget. External conditions (market, competition).
3. **Revenue Plan** — Revenue targets by segment/product. Growth rate and key assumptions.
4. **Revenue Bridge** — How we get from current run rate to plan: new business, expansion, churn assumptions.
5. **Gross Margin Plan** — Margin targets, key levers, cost of revenue build.
6. **Operating Expense Plan** — Total OpEx by function. Year-over-year change.
7. **Headcount Plan** — By department. Total hires, backfills, open roles by quarter.
8. **Investment Priorities** — Top 3–5 areas of incremental investment and why.
9. **What We're Not Investing In** — Deliberate trade-offs. Shows prioritization.
10. **Cash Flow + Runway** — Burn rate, cash position trajectory, key milestones.
11. **Sensitivity Analysis** — What happens if revenue is 10–20% below plan. Contingency actions.
12. **Key Assumptions** — Full list of planning assumptions. Makes the plan auditable.
13. **Decision + Approval** — What you're asking the board/exec to approve.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Revenue bridge from current run rate to plan
- Gross margin trajectory with cost drivers
- Headcount plan with quarterly hiring cadence
- Named investment priorities with strategic rationale
- Sensitivity analysis: what happens if revenue misses by 10–20%
- Full assumptions list — makes it easy to revisit mid-year

## Must-Avoid
- Budget presentations that add % to last year without strategic logic
- Plans with no sensitivity or downside scenario
- Headcount plans that are separate from the revenue plan
- Opex slides without year-over-year comparison
- Budget that shows only spending, not return on each investment

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Revenue plan: $ and growth %
- Gross margin target %
- Operating expense total and as % of revenue
- Total headcount: start of year, end of year, net adds
- Cash burn: monthly and annual
- Key milestone tied to the plan (e.g. ARR target, product launch date)

---

## Example Outline (Series B SaaS — FY2025 Budget)

1. FY2025 Budget Plan — [Company]
2. Context: 3 Strategic Priorities Driving This Plan
3. Revenue Plan: $13.2M ARR (61% YoY Growth)
4. Revenue Bridge: Run Rate + New Business + Expansion – Churn
5. Gross Margin: 72% → 74% — Cost of Revenue Plan
6. Operating Expenses: $[X]M — By Function, vs. FY2024
7. Headcount: 96 → 134 — Hiring Plan by Quarter + Department
8. Investment Priorities: GTM Scale, Product AI Features, CS Expansion
9. What We're Not Funding: [Deliberate Trade-offs]
10. Cash Flow: Peak Burn $[X]M/Month, 16-Month Runway
11. Sensitivity: –15% Revenue Scenario — Response Plan
12. Key Assumptions
13. Board Approval Requested
