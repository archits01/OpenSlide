# Seed / Pre-Seed Investor Pitch Deck

**What it is:** A fundraising deck for pre-seed or seed stage — making the case for team, market, and early product or insight before substantial traction exists.

---

## Audience
Angel investors, pre-seed/seed VCs, and accelerator programs. They're betting on the team and the thesis more than traction. They evaluate founder conviction, domain expertise, market selection, and early signals.

## Tone
Visionary and grounded. Seed decks should convey founder conviction without being delusional. Show you understand the market deeply, you've talked to customers, and you know what you don't know yet.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, tagline, founders
2. **The Problem** — What pain you're solving. Evidence it exists and is serious.
3. **Insight** — The non-obvious observation or mechanism that makes your solution better. Your unfair insight.
4. **The Solution** — What you're building. Current state + direction.
5. **Product / Demo** — What exists today. Even if MVP or prototype — show something.
6. **Market** — Market size with a bottom-up sizing. Why this market, why now.
7. **Business Model** — How you'll make money. Even if early hypothesis.
8. **Traction / Validation** — Letters of intent, design partners, pilots, waitlist, early revenue. What signal you have.
9. **Go-to-Market** — How you plan to acquire the first 50 customers.
10. **Team** — Why you (this team, this problem). Relevant background, domain expertise.
11. **What We've Learned** — Key experiments run, hypotheses validated or invalidated. Shows intellectual rigor.
12. **The Ask** — How much you're raising, what you'll prove with it, timeline to next milestone.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- The "insight" slide — what's the non-obvious thing you understand that others don't
- Customer evidence: interviews, LOIs, or early users — even 5–10 shows you've tested assumptions
- Why now: market timing argument
- Specific use of proceeds with milestones
- Team: why this team, not just credentials
- Honest "what we don't know yet"

## Must-Avoid
- Seed decks that look like Series A decks with no numbers filled in
- Market sizing without a bottom-up story
- Traction slides that show "100,000 signups" without any engagement or revenue
- Team slides with only titles and school names
- Hiding that you're pre-revenue — investors know, and transparency builds trust

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Design partners or pilot customers (even if unpaid)
- Estimated TAM with bottom-up methodology
- Round size and current valuation (or SAFE/note terms)
- Monthly burn rate (if operating)
- Milestone: what you'll achieve with this round
- Customer interview count (shows you've validated)

---

## Example Outline (Pre-Seed B2B SaaS)

1. [Company] — [One-Line Problem Statement]
2. The Problem: [Specific, Validated, Underserved]
3. Our Insight: Why Existing Approaches Fail
4. The Solution: What We're Building

7. Business Model: SaaS, $X/month, Annual Plans
8. Traction: 12 Design Partners, 3 Paid Pilots, $40K ARR
9. GTM: First 50 Customers Via [Specific Channel]

11. What We've Learned: 3 Hypotheses Tested
