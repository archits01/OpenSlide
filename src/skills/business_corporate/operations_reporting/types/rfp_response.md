# RFP Response Presentation

**What it is:** A structured presentation responding to a formal Request for Proposal — demonstrating capability, relevant experience, commercial terms, and why you should be selected.

---

## Audience
Procurement committee, technical evaluators, business owner, and often a scoring panel. They're evaluating multiple responses against defined criteria. Responses are often scored numerically.

## Tone
Professional, precise, and criteria-matched. Read the RFP requirements carefully — structure your response around their evaluation criteria, not your preferred story. Be specific and leave no requirement unanswered.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Your company name, RFP reference number, submission date
2. **Executive Summary** — Why you should win: 3 differentiating points, your understanding of their need.
3. **Our Understanding of Your Requirements** — Restate key requirements. Shows you read the brief.
4. **Our Capability: Overview** — Company size, relevant certifications, relevant contracts, geographies.
5. **Proposed Solution** — How you'll deliver what they need. Specific and detailed.
6. **Technical Approach** — Methodology, tools, systems, integration capabilities.
7. **Relevant Experience** — 3 comparable contracts/clients with outcomes. Match the RFP criteria.
8. **Team Proposed** — Named individuals, CVs summary, relevant experience.
9. **Implementation Plan** — Proposed timeline, phases, milestones, dependencies.
10. **Commercial Proposal** — Total cost, pricing structure, payment terms, any assumptions.
11. **Why Us** — 3 reasons you are the best choice. Specific to this RFP.
12. **Appendix** — Full CVs, financial accounts, references, certifications, T&Cs.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Executive summary that leads with your competitive advantage in this specific RFP
- Point-by-point response to stated requirements
- Named team members (procurement committees want to know who they're hiring)
- Comparable contract references that match the scale and scope of the RFP
- Commercial proposal with clear assumptions stated
- Compliance matrix (if required by RFP): all requirements mapped to your response

## Must-Avoid
- Generic company credentials that don't map to the specific requirements
- Commercial proposals without assumptions — creates post-award disputes
- Team slides showing the leadership team who won't work on the contract
- Responses that ignore requirements the RFP explicitly asked to address
- Overlong responses — procurement teams read dozens of bids

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Company revenue / size (for financial standing evaluation)
- Relevant contract count: similar scope/scale
- Proposed team FTEs and named individuals
- Total contract value (bid price)
- Timeline: proposed start date, key milestones
- Reference clients: company name, contract size, duration

---

## Example Outline (Professional Services RFP Response)

1. [Company Name] — Response to [Client] RFP [Reference]
2. Executive Summary: Why [Company] Is the Right Partner
3. Our Understanding: Your 5 Core Requirements
4. About [Company]: Scale, Certifications, Relevant Clients
5. Proposed Solution: How We'll Deliver [Core Service]
6. Our Technical Approach: Methodology + Tools
7. Relevant Experience: 3 Comparable Engagements with Outcomes
8. Proposed Team: Named Leads + Experience Summary
9. Implementation Plan: Phases, Milestones, Dependencies
10. Commercial Proposal: [£X] — Assumptions + Exclusions
11. Why [Company]: 3 Differentiators for This Contract
12. Appendix: CVs, References, Certifications
