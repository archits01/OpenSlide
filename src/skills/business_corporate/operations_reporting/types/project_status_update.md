# Project Status Update Presentation

**What it is:** A structured progress report on an active project — covering current status, milestones hit, blockers, budget/timeline health, and decisions required.

---

## Audience
Project sponsors, steering committee, or executive leadership. They don't have time for narrative — they need to quickly understand: are we on track, what's at risk, and what do they need to decide?

## Tone
Clear, factual, and direct. Use RAG (Red/Amber/Green) status. Surface problems early — the worst outcome is a surprise failure. Status updates that only show green are not trusted.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Project name, status date, project lead
2. **Overall Status** — One-page RAG summary: schedule, budget, scope, resources. Plus one sentence per dimension.
3. **Progress This Period** — What was completed since last update. Specific deliverables, not effort.
4. **Milestones: On Track / Slipped** — Timeline view. Color-coded. Slipped milestones with revised dates.
5. **Blockers + Risks** — Named blockers with owner, impact, and resolution path. Named risks with likelihood.
6. **Budget Status** — Spend to date vs. budget. Forecast to complete. Any variance explained.
7. **Scope Changes** — Any changes to scope since baseline. Impact on timeline and budget.
8. **Next Period Plan** — What will be completed in the next period. Named deliverables + dates.
9. **Decisions Required** — Explicit list of decisions the steering committee needs to make.
10. **Issues Log** — Appendix: full list of open issues with owner and status.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Overall RAG status prominently displayed — one color per dimension
- Slipped milestones with revised dates and root cause
- Named blockers — not just "there are some blockers"
- Budget vs. actuals with forecast to complete
- Decisions required slide — this is the most action-oriented slide

## Must-Avoid
- Status updates that are all green — it signals the presenter isn't being honest
- Progress described as effort ("we worked on X") rather than outcomes ("X was completed")
- Buried risks — they belong in the main deck
- Budget updates without explaining variances
- Ending without decisions required or next steps

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Overall project health: RAG per dimension
- % of milestones on track
- Budget: spent vs. total, % complete vs. % budget consumed
- Days until next major milestone
- Number of open blockers and risks

---

## Example Outline (Technology Implementation Project)

1. [Project Name] — Status Update: Week 14 of 24
2. Overall Status: AMBER — Schedule Risk, Budget Green
3. Progress This Week: [5 Completed Deliverables]
4. Milestone Tracker: 8 Complete, 2 Slipped, 6 Upcoming
5. Blockers: 3 Open — [Named, Owner, Resolution Path]
6. Budget: £[X] Spent of £[Y] (58% consumed, 63% schedule complete)
7. Scope: One Change Request Pending Approval
8. Next 2 Weeks: Planned Deliverables
9. Decisions Needed: [2 Items for Steering Committee]
10. Issues Log (Appendix)
