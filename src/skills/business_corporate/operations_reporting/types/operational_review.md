# Operational Review Presentation

**What it is:** A structured review of business operations across functions — presenting performance against operational KPIs, identifying inefficiencies, and setting operational improvement priorities.

---

## Audience
COO, CFO, functional heads, and executive leadership. Audience manages people and processes — they want actionable insights, not just metrics. Focus on what to fix and what to sustain.

## Tone
Analytical and systematic. Operational reviews should surface waste, inefficiency, and bottlenecks with the same rigor as financial reviews. Use benchmarking to contextualize performance.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, review period, presenter
2. **Operational Summary** — Overall operational health assessment. Key improvements and concerns.
3. **Company-Level KPIs** — 8–12 key operational metrics vs. target and vs. prior period.
4. **Process Efficiency** — Lead time, cycle time, error rate, rework. Trend view.
5. **Capacity + Resource Utilization** — Headcount utilization, system capacity, infrastructure headroom.
6. **Cost per Output** — Unit economics of key processes. Are we getting more efficient?
7. **Quality + Compliance** — Defect rates, SLA compliance, regulatory incidents.
8. **Customer Operations** — Response time, resolution rate, CSAT, escalation volume.
9. **Operational Risks** — Current operational risks: bottlenecks, single points of failure, pending risks.
10. **Improvement Initiatives: Status** — Active improvement projects with progress update.
11. **Next Period Priorities** — Top 3–5 operational improvement areas with owners.
12. **Investment Required** — Any budget or headcount needed to address identified issues.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- KPI table with targets and trend view
- Benchmarking against either industry or internal previous periods
- Explicit bottleneck or single point of failure identification
- Cost per output trend — efficiency improvement should be quantifiable
- Active improvement initiative status — shows operational management is working
- Next priorities with named owners

## Must-Avoid
- Operational reviews that are just reporting without analysis
- KPI tables without targets to compare against
- Identifying problems without proposing solutions
- Reviews that don't cover customer-facing operations
- Improvement initiatives without owners or timelines

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Primary operational KPIs vs. target (at least 5)
- Cost per output: current and trend
- SLA attainment %
- Error/defect rate trend
- Customer CSAT or response time
- Active improvement initiatives: count and status

---

## Example Outline (E-Commerce Operations Review)

1. Q1 Operational Review — [Company]
2. Operational Summary: Strong Logistics, Weak Returns Process
3. Key KPI Dashboard: 8 Metrics vs. Target
4. Process Efficiency: Order-to-Ship 2.1 Days (Target 1.8)
5. Capacity Utilization: Warehouse 78%, Inventory Turn 4.2×
6. Cost Per Order: $[X] (–8% YoY)
7. Quality: Defect Rate 1.2%, Returns Rate 8.4% (Industry 6%)
8. Customer Operations: NPS 42, Resolution Time 3.2 Days
9. Operational Risks: Returns Bottleneck, Carrier Concentration
10. Improvement Initiatives: 3 Active Projects — Status
11. Q2 Priorities: Returns Process, Carrier Diversification, Pick/Pack Efficiency
12. Investment: $[X]K for Returns Automation
