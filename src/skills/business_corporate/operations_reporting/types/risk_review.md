# Risk Review Presentation

**What it is:** A structured assessment of an organization's key risks — covering current risk exposure, controls in place, risk trajectory, and actions required from leadership.

---

## Audience
Board risk committee, audit committee, C-suite, or risk management leadership. Audience is responsible for risk oversight. They want assurance that risks are identified, monitored, and managed — with no surprises.

## Tone
Measured, precise, and structured. Risk reviews should be frank about exposure without being alarmist. Use consistent risk language and scoring so the audience can compare across periods and categories.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, risk review period, presented by risk/audit function
2. **Risk Register Overview** — Total risks in scope. Count by category and severity. Change since last review.
3. **Risk Heat Map** — Visual: likelihood × impact matrix with all material risks plotted.
4. **Top Risks: Deep Dive** — For each of the 5–8 most critical risks:
   - Risk description
   - Current controls
   - Residual risk rating
   - Trajectory (improving/stable/worsening)
   - Owner
5. **Risk Category Summaries** — Operational, financial, cyber, regulatory, strategic, reputational.
6. **Emerging Risks** — New risks added since last review. Early signals worth monitoring.
7. **Risks Closed / Improved** — Risks that have moved from high to medium/low. Shows risk management working.
8. **Controls Effectiveness** — Status of key controls. Any control failures in the period.
9. **Regulatory + Compliance Update** — Changes in regulatory landscape. Compliance deadlines.
10. **Management Actions Required** — Items requiring board or exec sign-off.
11. **Key Risk Indicators Dashboard** — The 8–12 metrics used to monitor risk level.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Risk heat map — visual representation of likelihood × impact
- Risk trajectory for each top risk (improving/stable/worsening)
- Named risk owners
- Controls effectiveness assessment
- Emerging risks section — shows forward-looking thinking
- Management actions required — the ask from the board

## Must-Avoid
- Risk registers that list 50+ risks without prioritization
- Risks with no owner assigned
- Risk reviews that show only current state without trajectory (are things getting better or worse?)
- Missing the emerging risks section — historical risks are the ones you already know about
- Board risk presentations without a clear ask from management

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Total risk count: high/medium/low
- Number of risks with improving/worsening trajectory
- Control failures in the period
- Key risk indicator values vs. thresholds
- Regulatory deadlines with days remaining

---

## Example Outline (Financial Services Risk Committee)

1. Q1 2025 Risk Review — Board Risk Committee
2. Risk Register Overview: 42 Active Risks, 8 High, 18 Medium, 16 Low
3. Risk Heat Map
4. Top 8 Risks: Deep Dive with Controls + Trajectory
5. Category Summaries: Operational, Cyber, Financial, Regulatory
6. Emerging Risks: [3 New Risk Areas Added This Quarter]
7. Risks Improved: 4 Moved from High to Medium
8. Controls Assessment: 2 Control Failures — Status + Remediation
9. Regulatory Update: DORA Compliance Deadline + Action Status
10. Key Risk Indicators Dashboard
11. Actions Required from Board: [Specific Items]
