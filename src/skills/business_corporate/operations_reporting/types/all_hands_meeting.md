# All-Hands Meeting Presentation

**What it is:** A company-wide or division-wide meeting deck — combining performance updates, strategic context, team news, and culture moments to align and energize the full organization.

---

## Audience
All employees across functions, levels, and locations. Audience ranges from interns to VPs. Must work for someone on their first week and someone who's been there 5 years.

## Tone
Transparent, energizing, and human. All-hands should feel like an honest conversation with the whole company, not a corporate broadcast. Mix hard facts with genuine recognition and forward direction.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, meeting name/quarter, date
2. **Agenda** — What we'll cover today. Keeps people oriented.
3. **Company Snapshot: Where We Are** — Headline numbers: revenue, customers, headcount, key milestones.
4. **What We Said We'd Do** — Goals set last quarter or last all-hands.
5. **What We Did** — Results vs. goals. Honest. Celebrate wins. Acknowledge misses.
6. **Challenges We're Working Through** — At least one honest challenge. Builds trust.
7. **Strategic Context** — What's happening in the market / industry that shapes our priorities.
8. **Priorities for Next Quarter** — 3–5 company-level priorities. Named owners.
9. **Team Updates** — New hires, promotions, team milestones. With names and faces.
10. **Customer Stories** — 1–2 customer wins or impact stories. Reminds people why the work matters.
11. **Recognition** — Spotlight 3–5 individuals or teams. Specific and sincere.
12. **Close + Q&A** — Open floor for questions. No "that's all we have time for" — make time.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- At least one honest acknowledgment of a challenge — all-hands that are only good news erode trust
- Results vs. stated goals — not just results in isolation
- Recognition with specific names and deeds
- Customer impact story — connects work to purpose
- A clear "what's next" section
- Genuine Q&A time — not a scripted close

## Must-Avoid
- All-hands that feel like investor presentations — employees need different information
- Recognition that lists 20+ people (dilutes impact) or zero people
- Jargon-heavy strategy slides that mean nothing to someone in support or operations
- Skipping the challenges section — it's the most important slide for culture
- Slides longer than 20 total — all-hands should be presentations, not lectures

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Revenue or ARR: current + growth vs. plan
- Customer count or key customer milestone
- Headcount: new hires this period
- Next quarter top priorities
- Key win (deal closed, feature shipped, award won)

---

## Example Outline (Series B SaaS — Monthly All-Hands)

1. [Company] All-Hands — February 2025
2. Today's Agenda
3. Where We Are: $8.2M ARR, 184 Customers, 96 People
4. What We Said in January
5. What Happened in January: Wins, Misses, Lessons
6. What's Hard Right Now: [Honest Challenge + Plan]
7. The Market Context: What We're Seeing
8. February Priorities: 4 Company-Level Focus Areas
9. Welcome + Promotions: [Names]
10. Customer Story: [Company X] Impact
11. Shoutouts: 5 People Who Made a Difference
12. Your Questions
