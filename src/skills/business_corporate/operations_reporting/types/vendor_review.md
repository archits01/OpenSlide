# Vendor / Supplier Review Presentation

**What it is:** A structured performance review of a vendor or supplier relationship — evaluating delivery against SLAs, costs, strategic value, and renewal/continuation decision.

---

## Audience
Procurement lead, operations director, CFO, and relevant business stakeholders. Audience is evaluating whether to renew, renegotiate, or replace the vendor. They want an objective assessment.

## Tone
Analytical and commercially rigorous. Avoid relationship language when evaluating performance — use data. Surface both strengths and failures honestly to support a sound commercial decision.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Vendor name, review period, contract value, decision date
2. **Relationship Summary** — Length of relationship, contract value, scope of services.
3. **SLA Performance** — Key SLAs vs. actual performance. Green/Red by metric.
4. **Quality / Output Review** — What was delivered vs. what was contracted.
5. **Cost Performance** — Actual spend vs. contracted. Any budget overruns or underruns. Cost per unit trend.
6. **Incident + Issue Log** — Material incidents, escalations, resolution quality.
7. **Strategic Value** — Beyond the SLA: does this vendor make us better? Innovation, insight, proactivity.
8. **Benchmarking** — How does this vendor compare to market alternatives? Pricing, SLA, service quality.
9. **Risk Assessment** — Dependency, concentration, vendor financial health, geo/regulatory risk.
10. **Vendor's Perspective** — Any feedback or concerns raised by the vendor. Shows balanced review.
11. **Options** — Renew as-is, renegotiate, partial replacement, full replacement with alternatives.
12. **Recommendation** — Recommended path with rationale and next steps.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- SLA performance table with actuals vs. targets
- Total cost and year-over-year trend
- At least one market benchmark — shows whether you're getting fair value
- Named incidents with resolution quality assessment
- Strategic value assessment — not just compliance with SLAs
- A clear recommendation: renew, renegotiate, replace

## Must-Avoid
- Vendor reviews that only focus on SLA compliance without strategic assessment
- Recommendations without considering switching costs
- Benchmarking skipped because "we've always used this vendor"
- Reviews that contain no negative feedback on strong relationships — credibility requires honesty
- Missing the risk section — vendor concentration is a real business risk

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Contract value: annual and total term
- SLA attainment: % by key metric
- Incidents: count, severity, resolution time
- Cost per unit trend (if applicable)
- Market benchmark: equivalent vendor pricing
- Switching cost estimate

---

## Example Outline (Cloud Infrastructure Vendor Review)

1. AWS Annual Vendor Review — FY2025
2. Relationship: 4 Years, $2.4M Annual Spend, Core Infrastructure
3. SLA Performance: 99.94% Uptime (Target 99.9%) — All Green
4. Output Review: Committed Optimization Projects — 3 of 5 Delivered
5. Cost: $2.4M Actual vs. $2.1M Budget — 14% Overage Analysis
6. Incident Review: 2 P1 Events, Resolution 4h and 11h
7. Strategic Value: Proactive Optimization Recommendations — High
8. Market Benchmark: GCP and Azure Equivalent Pricing Comparison
9. Risk Assessment: High Dependency, Mitigation via Multi-Region
10. Vendor's Perspective: Migration Support Request
11. Options: Renew, Renegotiate Cost Structure, Partial Migration to GCP
12. Recommendation: Renew + Renegotiate Committed Use Discounts
