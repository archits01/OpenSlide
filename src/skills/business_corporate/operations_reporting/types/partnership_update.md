# Partnership Update Presentation

**What it is:** A structured update on the state of a strategic partnership — covering joint activities, performance vs. targets, relationship health, and next steps for the partnership.

---

## Audience
Internal exec team and/or the partner organization's senior leadership. Audience wants to understand whether the partnership is delivering value and what needs to change.

## Tone
Collaborative but rigorous. Partnership reviews should be commercially honest — if targets aren't being hit, say so clearly along with the analysis and proposed remediation.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Partnership name (Company A + Company B), review period, date
2. **Partnership Overview** — Objective of the partnership. What both parties expected.
3. **What We Committed To** — Mutual commitments: targets, activities, investments.
4. **Performance vs. Targets** — Joint KPIs vs. plan. Be honest about misses.
5. **Joint Activities Completed** — What was executed: co-sell activities, events, integrations, marketing.
6. **Pipeline / Revenue Impact** — Pipeline generated through partnership channel. Revenue closed.
7. **Highlights + Wins** — Specific joint wins. Named deals or customer outcomes.
8. **What's Not Working** — Honest assessment of gaps: lack of engagement, misaligned incentives, execution gaps.
9. **Root Cause Analysis** — Why the gaps exist. Systemic or one-off.
10. **Next Period Plan** — Specific joint activities planned. Targets for next period.
11. **Changes Required** — If the partnership needs to change: different structure, new incentives, additional resources.
12. **Mutual Asks** — What each party needs from the other to improve outcomes.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Original partnership commitments for reference
- Pipeline and revenue attributed to the partnership channel
- Joint win highlights with customer names (if permissible)
- Honest "what's not working" section
- Mutual asks from both sides — not just one-way ask
- Next period targets with specific activities

## Must-Avoid
- Partnership reviews that are just activity reports without commercial outcomes
- Skipping the "what's not working" section in review meetings
- Pipeline numbers without source attribution (were these deals that would have happened anyway?)
- Vague next period plans ("continue to collaborate")
- Leaving without agreed mutual commitments

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Pipeline generated through partnership: $ total
- Revenue closed: $ 
- Joint activities: events, co-sell sessions, referrals made
- Partner-sourced revenue as % of total revenue
- Target for next period: pipeline and revenue

---

## Example Outline (Technology Company + System Integrator)

1. [Company] × [Partner] Quarterly Partnership Review — Q1 2025
2. Partnership Objective: Build a $2M Partner Channel in 12 Months
3. Q1 Commitments: Targets + Mutual Activities
4. Q1 Performance: $340K Pipeline (Target $500K), 2 Closed Deals
5. Q1 Joint Activities: 2 Events, 8 Co-Sell Calls, 1 Integration

11. Proposed Changes: Revised Incentive Model
12. Mutual Asks: [Company] Needs → [Partner] Needs
