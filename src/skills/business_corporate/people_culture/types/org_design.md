# Org Design / Restructuring Presentation

**What it is:** A presentation proposing or communicating an organizational design change — covering the rationale, new structure, role impacts, transition plan, and expected outcomes.

---

## Audience
Executive leadership team and/or the broader organization (if it's a communication). Audience ranges from anxious employees (if their jobs are affected) to analytical executives (evaluating structural logic).

## Tone
Clear, purposeful, and empathetic. Org change creates anxiety — the presentation should acknowledge that directly. Lead with the why before the what. Be specific about impacts.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, change title (e.g. "Organizational Redesign — Q3 2025"), date
2. **Why We're Changing** — Strategic or operational driver. Not "to improve efficiency" — be specific.
3. **What Isn't Working Today** — Honest diagnosis of the current structure's limitations.
4. **Design Principles** — The criteria used to evaluate the new structure (e.g. "customer-facing teams organized around segments").
5. **The New Structure** — Org chart. New reporting lines, team composition, span of control.
6. **What Changes for Each Team** — By function or department: new scope, new leader, new peers.
7. **Role Impacts** — If roles are eliminated, changed, or created: number, type, timeline.
8. **Transition Plan** — How we get from current to new state. Timeline, critical path.
9. **New Leadership Assignments** — Named leaders in new or changed roles with rationale.
10. **What This Enables** — The strategic or operational outcomes the new design unlocks.
11. **What Stays the Same** — Reassurance slide: what isn't changing.
12. **Your Questions** — Open Q&A. Allow time.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Strategic rationale for the change — specific, not generic "efficiency" framing
- Current structure limitations named honestly
- Design principles used to evaluate the new structure
- Role impact numbers — even if painful, people need the facts
- Transition plan with timeline
- Named leaders in new structure with rationale
- A "what stays the same" slide — anchors stability

## Must-Avoid
- Org design presentations that announce structure without rationale
- Presentations that bury role elimination in vague language
- No transition timeline — creates anxiety
- Missing the "what this enables" slide — every change needs a purpose
- No Q&A time — this is the most important part of a restructuring communication

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Number of teams/functions affected
- Role changes: new roles created, roles eliminated, roles changed
- Transition timeline: days/weeks to new structure
- Reporting structure changes: number of reporting line changes
- Number of employees affected

---

## Example Outline (180-Person PR Consultancy Restructure)

1. Organizational Redesign: Building for the Next Phase
2. Why We're Changing: Our Current Structure Limits How We Serve Clients
3. What's Not Working: 3 Structural Limitations
4. Our Design Principles: Client-Centric, Clear Accountability, Talent Mobility
5. The New Structure [Org Chart]
6. What Changes for Each Practice
7. Role Impacts: 4 New Roles, 2 Restructured, 0 Eliminations
8. Transition Timeline: Effective June 1
9. New Leadership: [Named Roles + People]
10. What This Unlocks: Client Experience + Speed
11. What Stays the Same: Your Teams, Your Work, Your Manager
12. Your Questions
