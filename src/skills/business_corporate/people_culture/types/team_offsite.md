# Team Offsite / Retreat Presentation

**What it is:** A working session or strategic offsite presentation — designed to achieve team alignment on goals, relationships, or strategy in a focused off-site environment.

---

## Audience
A leadership team, department team, or cross-functional group. Audience has invested time and travel to be together — they want the time to be genuinely productive, not another meeting that could have been a video call.

## Tone
Energizing and candid. Offsites should create space for conversations that don't happen in normal working hours. Tone should be more relaxed than a board meeting but focused enough to produce real output.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Team name, offsite name, location, dates
2. **Why We're Here** — Goals for the offsite. What we need to leave with.
3. **Where We Are: Team Check-In** — How is the team doing? Open honest reflection.
4. **What We Accomplished** — Wins from the past period. Celebration and acknowledgment.
5. **What We're Working Through** — Honest challenges the team is facing collectively.
6. **Our Goals for [Next Period]** — What we're committing to together.
7. **How We Work Together** — Team operating principles, norms, decision-making.
8. **Working Session: [Topic 1]** — Facilitated discussion or working session.
9. **Working Session: [Topic 2]** — Second working session.
10. **What We Decided** — Capture decisions made during the offsite.
11. **Commitments + Owners** — Individual and collective commitments from the session.
12. **What We Want to Take Forward** — Anything about the offsite dynamic worth preserving.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Explicit goals for the offsite — what success looks like
- Space for the team to be honest about challenges — not just a celebration
- Working sessions with facilitated output — not just presentations
- Decisions captured explicitly
- Individual commitments with owners and timelines
- Enough social time — offsites that are 100% structured don't achieve relationship building

## Must-Avoid
- Offsite agendas that are back-to-back presentations without working time
- No explicit goals — offsites without a purpose waste investment
- Decisions that aren't captured and shared after
- Failing to address team tensions that exist — they surface themselves in the format anyway
- Presentations that could just be read — offsite time is for discussion, not delivery

---

## Key Data Points to Include (context)
- Team size and remote/distributed nature (explains why offsite is needed)
- Frequency of offsites (sets expectations)
- Outcomes the offsite needs to produce
- Key decisions or topics that must be resolved

---

## Example Outline (Leadership Team Offsite — 2 Days)

1. [Company] Leadership Offsite — July 2025
2. Why We're Here: 3 Goals for These 2 Days
3. Team Check-In: How Are We Actually Doing?
4. Celebrating Q2: What We're Proud Of
5. Being Honest: Where We're Stuck or Misaligned
6. H2 Priorities: Aligning on What Matters
7. How This Team Works Best: Norms + Commitments
8. Working Session 1: [Strategic Topic — e.g. Enterprise Motion]
9. Working Session 2: [Operational Topic — e.g. Hiring Plan]
10. Decisions Made
11. Individual Commitments: What Each Person Is Taking Back
12. What We Want to Keep From This Offsite
