# Employer Branding Presentation

**What it is:** A presentation defining or refreshing the company's employer value proposition (EVP) — covering what makes the company a great place to work, how that's communicated externally, and the talent acquisition strategy.

---

## Audience
People leadership, CMO, CEO, and recruiters. Audience is approving the EVP framework and talent marketing strategy. They want to see evidence that the EVP is grounded in what employees actually experience, not just aspirational.

## Tone
Strategic and candid. Employer branding built on false premises fails — candidates discover the truth in interviews and early employment. Ground the EVP in real employee research, then build authentic communications around it.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, EVP refresh/launch title, date
2. **Why Employer Branding Matters Now** — Business case: hiring velocity, offer acceptance rate, quality of candidates.
3. **Current State: How We're Perceived** — What candidates say, Glassdoor data, offer acceptance rate, exit interview themes.
4. **Employee Research: What People Love** — Survey data, focus group themes: top reasons people stay and recommend working here.
5. **Employee Research: What We Need to Improve** — Honest gaps. Shows the EVP is grounded in reality.
6. **Our Employer Value Proposition** — The EVP statement. What makes this company uniquely valuable as an employer.
7. **EVP Pillars** — 3–5 themes that support the EVP. Each with evidence.
8. **Target Talent Profiles** — Personas for the talent we're trying to attract. What motivates them.
9. **Content + Channel Strategy** — Where we'll communicate the EVP and how (LinkedIn, Glassdoor, careers page, events).
10. **Investment Required** — Budget for careers page, content, events, tools.
11. **Metrics** — How we'll measure EVP effectiveness: offer acceptance, time-to-fill, quality of hire, Glassdoor score.
12. **Timeline** — EVP launch and ongoing employer brand activation plan.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Employee research evidence behind the EVP — not invented from leadership's wishlist
- Honest "what we need to improve" slide — critical for credibility
- EVP statement that's specific to this company (not generic)
- Target talent personas with what motivates them
- Metrics to measure EVP effectiveness
- Budget tied to specific tactics

## Must-Avoid
- EVPs built without employee research ("management decided what the EVP should be")
- Generic EVP pillars that could apply to any company
- Channel plans without channel rationale
- Employer branding as purely a marketing exercise — it must connect to the actual employee experience
- Missing the "what we need to improve" section

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Current offer acceptance rate
- Glassdoor rating and trend
- Candidate NPS or recruiter feedback
- Employee NPS (eNPS)
- Time-to-fill: key roles
- Budget request: total and breakdown
