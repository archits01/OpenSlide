# Culture Deck

**What it is:** A presentation of the company's values, working principles, and cultural identity — used externally for employer branding and internally to align and reinforce cultural expectations.

---

## Audience
External: candidates, prospective employees, partners. Internal: new hires, all employees, leadership. Audience wants to understand what it's actually like to work here — not what the handbook says.

## Tone
Authentic, specific, and confident. The best culture decks are opinionated — they're honest about who thrives here and who doesn't. Avoid HR-speak. Write how people actually talk.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, deck title (e.g. "How We Work at [Company]")
2. **Who We Are** — Mission and what we actually do. One paragraph max.
3. **Why Culture Matters Here** — The business case for a strong culture at this company specifically.
4. **Our Values** — 4–6 values. Each with:
   - The value in a phrase
   - What it means in practice (specific behaviors)
   - What it doesn't mean (prevent misinterpretation)
5. **How We Work** — Operating principles: how decisions are made, how we communicate, how we handle disagreement.
6. **What We Hire For** — The profile of someone who thrives here. Honest about what we select on.
7. **What We Don't Tolerate** — Non-negotiable behavioral standards. Named, not vague.
8. **How We Think About Performance** — Feedback culture, recognition, accountability model.
9. **What We're Building Toward** — The kind of company we're trying to become.
10. **What This Isn't** — Explicit about things we're NOT (not a big company, not a place for X type of person).

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Values that are specific enough to be actionable — not just words
- "What it doesn't mean" for each value — prevents lazy application
- "What we don't tolerate" slide — the clearest signal of real values
- Who thrives here vs. who doesn't — honesty builds trust with candidates
- Specific behaviors, not abstract principles

## Must-Avoid
- Value words that every company uses ("integrity," "innovation," "teamwork") without specificity
- Culture decks that read like employee handbooks
- Presenting aspirational culture as current reality
- Missing the "what we don't tolerate" section — it's the most important slide
- Anything that sounds like it was written by a committee

---

## Key Data Points to Include (context, not metrics)
- Company size and growth stage
- Remote/hybrid/in-office model
- Decision-making model (how authority works)
- Performance review approach (360, manager-led, frequency)
- How long the company has been operating on these values

---

## Example Outline (Series B SaaS Company)

1. How We Work at [Company]
2. What We're Building and Why It Matters
3. Culture Is How We Compete
4. Our 5 Values: What They Mean and What They Don't
5. How We Make Decisions
6. Who Thrives Here: The Profile We Hire and Promote For
7. What We Don't Tolerate
8. How We Give Feedback and Recognize Performance
9. The Company We're Trying to Become
10. This Isn't for Everyone — And That's Intentional
