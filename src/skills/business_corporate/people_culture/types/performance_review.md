# Performance Review Presentation

**What it is:** A structured presentation of performance review findings, outcomes, or system design — either communicating the results of a review cycle or presenting a new performance management framework.

---

## Audience
Leadership team (for cycle results) or all employees (for framework introduction). Audience wants clarity: what was measured, how, what happens next, and what it means for them.

## Tone
Clear, fair-minded, and specific. Performance communication must balance individual sensitivity with organizational transparency. Avoid vague language that obscures what's actually being communicated.

---

## Slide Structure (for Framework Introduction)

1. **Cover** — Company name, performance review cycle name, date
2. **Why We're Here** — Purpose of the review cycle. What it's designed to achieve.
3. **The Framework** — What's being evaluated: goals, behaviors, growth. How it's structured.
4. **The Process** — Timeline, steps, who completes what.
5. **How Ratings Work** — Rating scale, definitions, calibration process.
6. **How Compensation Links** — How performance outcomes connect to salary, bonus, or equity.
7. **Calibration** — How cross-team fairness is maintained.
8. **What Good Feedback Looks Like** — Examples of specific vs. vague feedback.
9. **Your Role in the Process** — What each employee and manager does.
10. **Timeline + Key Dates** — Deadline for self-reviews, manager reviews, calibration, results communication.
11. **Resources** — Where to find training, examples, and support.

---

## Slide Structure (for Cycle Results — Leadership)

1. **Cover** — Company name, review cycle, results date
2. **Cycle Overview** — Participation rate, completion rate.
3. **Performance Distribution** — % in each rating category. Vs. targets.
4. **Performance by Team/Department** — Distribution breakdown.
5. **High Performers** — Actions being taken to recognize and retain.
6. **Performance Concerns** — Number of employees with improvement plans. Actions.
7. **Compensation Outcomes** — Budget, % of employees receiving increases, average increase %.
8. **Promotion Decisions** — Number and function.
9. **Insights + Themes** — What the data tells us about our talent and culture.
10. **Actions for Next Cycle** — What we're changing based on this cycle.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Clear timeline with specific dates
- Rating scale with explicit definitions — not just "meets/exceeds"
- Calibration explanation — the most common employee concern
- Compensation linkage — employees need to know how this affects pay
- Examples of good feedback
- For results: honest distribution data, not just aggregate

## Must-Avoid
- Rating scales without behavioral definitions
- Process presentations that skip the compensation link
- Timeline with only one date ("complete by X")
- Calibration described as "managers discuss" without explaining the process
- Results presentations that aggregate everything and hide distribution

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Participation rate: % of employees completing reviews
- Rating distribution: % in each category
- Compensation budget: $ and % of salary
- Number of promotions
- Timeline: key dates for each step
