# DEI Presentation

**What it is:** A presentation on diversity, equity, and inclusion — covering current state representation data, commitments, progress against targets, and the business case.

---

## Audience
Board, all-hands, external stakeholders, or ERG leadership. Audience varies: the board needs accountability data; all-hands needs inspiration and honesty; external audiences need credible transparency.

## Tone
Honest, action-oriented, and undefensive. DEI presentations that only show positive progress erode trust. Acknowledge where representation is weak, set specific targets, and report progress at every update.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, DEI report/update title, period
2. **Why We're Here** — Business and ethical rationale. Why DEI is a strategic priority at this company.
3. **Where We Are: Representation Data** — Full breakdown by gender, ethnicity, level. Don't hide the gaps.
4. **Representation by Level** — Where diversity drops off in the org (often at senior levels).
5. **Hiring + Pipeline Data** — Candidate diversity at application, interview, offer, and hire stages.
6. **Pay Equity** — Pay gap analysis by gender and ethnicity. Methodology stated.
7. **Culture + Belonging** — eNPS by demographic group if available. Inclusion survey results.
8. **What We've Done** — Concrete actions taken in the review period.
9. **What Didn't Work** — Honest assessment of initiatives that didn't move the needle.
10. **Our Commitments for [Next Period]** — Specific, measurable targets with timelines and owners.
11. **How We'll Be Held Accountable** — Who tracks this, how it's reported, who owns outcomes.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Representation data broken down by gender and ethnicity, at minimum
- Representation by level — showing where the pipeline breaks down
- Pay equity analysis with methodology
- At least one honest "what didn't work" item
- Specific measurable targets (not "improve diversity")
- Named accountability owner for each commitment

## Must-Avoid
- Representation data without year-over-year comparison
- Commitments without measurable targets or timelines
- DEI presentations that are all progress — at least acknowledge current gaps
- Pay equity data without explanation of methodology (median vs. mean, like-for-like vs. unadjusted)
- Treating DEI as a communications exercise rather than a structural change programme

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Gender representation: % by level
- Ethnic minority representation: % by level
- Gender pay gap: median unadjusted
- Candidate diversity at each hiring stage
- eNPS by demographic group (if available)
- YoY change in representation at senior levels

---

## Example Outline (Mid-Size Tech Company — Annual DEI Report)

1. [Company] DEI Annual Report 2025
2. Our Commitment: Why This Matters at [Company]
3. Representation Today: Full Org Breakdown
4. Where the Pipeline Breaks: Representation by Level
5. Hiring Funnel: Where Diversity Is Lost
6. Pay Equity: Gender and Ethnicity Gap Analysis
7. Belonging: eNPS by Group, Inclusion Survey Findings
8. What We Did in 2024: Concrete Actions
9. What We Tried That Didn't Work
10. Our 2025 Commitments: 5 Specific Targets with Owners
11. Accountability: How Progress Is Tracked and Reported
