# Onboarding Deck (New Hire Orientation)

**What it is:** A structured presentation for new employees in their first week — orienting them to the company, culture, tools, expectations, and team.

---

## Audience
New hires across all roles and levels. Audience is anxious and overwhelmed with information. They want to feel welcome, understand the context, and know what's expected of them.

## Tone
Welcoming, clear, and practical. Onboarding should reduce anxiety, not add to it. Prioritize the "what matters for day 1" over the "everything you need to know." Save depth for later sessions.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Cover** — Company name, "Welcome to [Company]", date
2. **Welcome + What This Is** — Agenda and what we'll cover. Sets expectations.
3. **Who We Are + What We Do** — Company mission, product, customers. The 2-minute version.
4. **Our Customers** — Who we serve and the impact we have. Connects new hires to purpose.
5. **How We Work** — Operating model: remote/hybrid, communication norms, decision-making.
6. **Our Values in Practice** — Values as behaviors. How they show up day-to-day.
7. **Your Team** — Who's in the room or on the team. Faces, roles, how to reach people.
8. **Tools + Systems** — The essential stack: email, Slack, JIRA, HR system. Where to find help.
9. **Your First 30/60/90 Days** — What's expected and what you'll learn at each stage.
10. **Admin + HR Essentials** — Benefits, policies, payroll setup, time-off process.
11. **How to Get Help** — Who to ask about what. Buddy program, manager, People Ops.
12. **Your First Actions** — Specific checklist for today and this week.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- A genuine welcome — not a corporate header
- Company purpose connected to customer impact (not just mission statement)
- Values as specific behaviors, not words
- 30/60/90-day expectations — reduces new hire anxiety dramatically
- Named buddy or go-to person for the first month
- First-week checklist — specific actions reduce overwhelm

## Must-Avoid
- Onboarding decks that are 50+ slides — they become information dumps
- Values slides that are just words or slogans without behavioral context
- Skipping the "how to get help" slide — new hires are afraid to ask for help
- Admin-heavy sessions before culture and purpose — sequence matters
- No indication of what's expected in the first 90 days

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Company size, founding year, brief history milestone
- Key customer names or sectors (makes company real)
- Team size for the new hire's function
- First week key dates (1:1 with manager, team meeting, etc.)
- Benefits enrollment deadline

---

## Example Outline (Series B SaaS Company)

1. Welcome to [Company]!
2. What We'll Cover Today
3. What We Do and Why It Matters
4. Our Customers: Who We Serve
5. How We Work: Remote-First, Async-Friendly
6. Our Values in Practice: What They Look Like Day-to-Day
7. Your Team: Who You're Working With
8. Your Essential Tools: Slack, Notion, Jira, BambooHR
9. Your First 30/60/90 Days: What Good Looks Like
10. Benefits, Payroll + HR Admin
11. How to Get Help: Your Buddy, Your Manager, People Ops
12. Your First Week Checklist
