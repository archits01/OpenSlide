# OKR / Goal-Setting Presentation

**What it is:** A planning session or communication deck that introduces, cascades, or reviews OKRs across the company — aligning teams on what matters and how success is measured.

---

## Audience
Leadership team, department heads, and/or the full company. Audience varies by purpose: exec review (strategic) vs. all-hands (alignment) vs. team planning (operational).

## Tone
Clear, direct, energizing. OKR sessions often suffer from corporate blandness — good tone is ambitious but grounded. Acknowledge what didn't work before proposing the new approach.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Title** — Company name, planning cycle (e.g. "2025 Annual OKR Planning")
2. **What We're Here to Do** — Purpose of the session. Decision vs. inform vs. review.
3. **Last Cycle Review** — What we set, what we achieved. % attainment. Honest assessment.
4. **What We Learned** — Root causes of misses, patterns across teams, what to fix this cycle.
5. **Our Framework This Cycle** — Rules: max # OKRs, cascading logic, check-in cadence, scoring method.
6. **Company-Level OKRs** — 3 max. Each with key results that are measurable and time-bound.
7. **OKR Cascade: How Departments Connect** — Visual showing how team OKRs support company OKRs.
8. **[Department] OKRs** — Repeat for each major team. Lead with department, then OKRs with KRs.
9. **Cross-Functional Dependencies** — OKRs that require alignment across 2+ teams. Named owners.
10. **Check-In Rhythm** — When we review, how we score, what triggers re-prioritization.
11. **How to Write a Good Key Result** — Brief coaching slide. Examples of weak vs. strong KRs.
12. **Next Steps + Calendar** — What each team does next and by when.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Previous cycle attainment data — honest, not glossed over
- Company-level OKRs (max 3) with specific key results
- Named owners for every OKR
- Cross-functional dependency map
- Explicit rules: max OKR count, scoring method, check-in cadence
- Calendar of review points

## Must-Avoid
- More than 3 company-level OKRs — it signals everything is a priority
- Key results that are tasks ("launch new feature") not outcomes ("increase X by Y%")
- OKR slides with no numerical targets
- Skipping the retrospective on last cycle — it's the most important slide
- Department OKRs that don't visibly connect to company OKRs

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Previous cycle attainment: overall % + breakdown by team
- Number of OKRs set last cycle (if reducing, show the before/after)
- Company OKRs: 3 objectives with 2–3 measurable key results each
- Review cadence: monthly/quarterly check-in dates
- OKR count limits: per team and per person

1. Flowdesk Annual OKR Planning 2025
