# SWOT / Strategic Review Presentation

**What it is:** A structured strategic review combining internal assessment (strengths/weaknesses) with external analysis (opportunities/threats) to generate strategic choices and priorities.

---

## Audience
Board, senior leadership team, or strategy committee. Used in periodic reviews (annual, triennial) to reframe strategy. Audience wants clarity on where to invest, where to protect, and where to exit.

## Tone
Candid and evidence-based. SWOT sessions often produce generic outputs — the value is in specificity and intellectual honesty. Name uncomfortable truths: client concentration, talent gaps, competitive threats.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Title** — Company name, review scope (e.g. "Triennial Strategic Review 2025"), presenter
2. **Purpose and Process** — What this review covered, how it was conducted, who contributed
3. **Where We Are: Performance Snapshot** — Revenue, profitability, growth, key operational metrics vs. goals
4. **Strengths** — What genuinely differentiates us. Named, specific, supported by evidence. Not just "great team."
5. **Weaknesses** — Honest internal gaps. Each with an impact assessment.
6. **Opportunities** — External trends or market gaps we're positioned to capture. With sizing where possible.
7. **Threats** — External risks that could undermine the business model. Named competitors, trends, structural risks.
8. **SWOT Matrix** — One-slide visual summary. Crosses: SO (use strengths to capture opportunities), ST, WO, WT strategies.
9. **Strategic Choices** — What the SWOT analysis tells us to do. 3–4 strategic moves derived from the matrix.
10. **Investment Implications** — Where to invest more, where to maintain, where to reduce or exit
11. **Risks If We Don't Act** — Cost of inaction for top 2 threats and top 2 weaknesses
12. **Recommendations + Next Steps** — Specific decisions and owners. Not "further analysis required."

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Specific strengths with evidence (client retention rate, NPS, revenue share)
- Named weaknesses with impact quantification (client concentration %, market gap)
- Opportunities with market sizing where possible
- A SWOT matrix that generates strategic choices, not just lists
- Investment implications — what this review means for resource allocation
- A recommendations section with named owners and timelines

## Must-Avoid
- SWOT lists that are generic (strength: "experienced team", weakness: "limited resources")
- Opportunities listed without any basis for why we're positioned to capture them
- A threats section that lists everything without prioritization
- SWOT that doesn't lead to strategic choices — it's an analysis tool, not an output
- Reviews that avoid naming the uncomfortable realities (key client dependency, competitive weakness)

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Client concentration: top 3 clients as % of revenue
- Average client relationship length
- Revenue growth rate vs. market growth rate
- Gross margin trend
- Market share estimate
- Competitor growth trajectory (if available)

---

## Example Outline (Independent PR Consultancy — Triennial Review)

1. Triennial Strategic Review 2025: Horizon III
2. Process: How We Conducted This Review
3. Where We Stand: Revenue, Margin, Client Retention
4. Our Strengths: B2B Tech Depth, Retained Model, 4.2-Year Avg Relationships
5. Our Weaknesses: No US Presence, 42% Revenue in 3 Clients
6. Opportunities: AI/Tech PR Boom, M&A in Our Category, Talent Availability
7. Threats: In-House Team Growth, Big Agency Consolidation, Tech Budget Cuts
8. SWOT Matrix: Strategic Choices
9. Strategic Moves: International Expansion, Client Diversification, AI Practice
10. Investment Implications: Where to Grow, Where to Protect
11. Cost of Inaction: What Happens If We Don't Address Concentration Risk
12. Recommendations + Owner Assignments
