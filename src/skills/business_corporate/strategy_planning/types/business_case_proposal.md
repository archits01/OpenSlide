# Business Case / Investment Proposal

**What it is:** A structured argument for allocating budget or resources to a specific initiative — showing the problem, the solution, the expected return, and the risks.

---

## Audience
CFO, CEO, CPO, or investment/capital committee. Competing against other proposals for the same budget. Audience is skeptical by default — they need to justify saying yes.

## Tone
Rigorous, confident, commercially fluent. Lead with the opportunity cost of not investing. Every claim should be defensible. Use the CFO's language: ROI, payback period, NPV, risk-adjusted return.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Title** — Initiative name, sponsoring team, investment ask, date
2. **The Problem We're Solving** — Quantified problem: revenue lost, cost incurred, risk exposure. Not just "a pain point."
3. **Why This, Why Now** — What changed that makes this urgent. Market shift, competitive threat, customer demand.
4. **The Proposed Solution** — What exactly we're building or doing. Scope in/out.
5. **Investment Required** — Total cost, breakdown by phase or category (people, technology, external). Timeline.
6. **Expected Return** — Revenue uplift, cost savings, or risk reduction. Model the base case and upside.
7. **Payback Period + ROI** — When does the investment break even? 3-year return model.
8. **How We'll Execute** — Phases, milestones, owners. Shows the plan is real.
9. **What Could Go Wrong** — Top 3 risks with likelihood and mitigation. Shows intellectual honesty.
10. **Alternatives Considered** — Build vs. buy vs. partner vs. do nothing. Why this option won.
11. **Our Ask** — Exact budget, headcount, timeline approval needed. Clear decision point.
12. **Appendix** — Detailed financial model, assumptions list, research sources.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Quantified problem statement with a £/$ cost to the business
- Return model with explicit assumptions (conversion lift %, revenue per customer, etc.)
- Payback period calculation — CFOs always ask
- Alternatives considered — shows diligence
- Risk section with mitigations — not just risks
- An explicit ask on the final slide with a decision date

## Must-Avoid
- Business cases that open with the solution before defining the problem
- ROI projections with no stated assumptions
- Scope that includes everything with no trade-offs
- A risk section that lists low-impact/low-probability risks to look thorough
- Ending without a specific decision ask — "for discussion" is not an ask

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Total investment: £/$ and time horizon
- Expected revenue uplift or cost saving: range (base/upside)
- Payback period: months
- Conversion rate or metric before/after
- Implementation timeline: phases and milestones
- Competing proposals this budget is being weighed against (if known)

---

## Example Outline (E-Commerce AI Personalization)

1. AI Personalization Engine: Investment Case — £2.1M Ask
2. The Problem: 2.8% Conversion Rate Costs Us £34M in Missed Revenue Annually
3. Why Now: Personalization Is No Longer Differentiation — It's Table Stakes
4. What We're Building: Phase 1–3 Scope + Exclusions
5. Investment: £2.1M Over 18 Months — People, Platform, Integration
6. Expected Return: 8–12% Conversion Lift = £22–34M Incremental Revenue
7. Payback Period: 14 Months (Base Case)
8. How We'll Execute: 3 Phases with Milestones + Owners
9. What Could Go Wrong: Data Quality, Integration Risk, Adoption
10. What We Considered: Build vs. Buy vs. Partner
11. The Ask: £2.1M Budget + 3 FTE Approval by [Date]
12. Appendix: Financial Model + Assumptions
