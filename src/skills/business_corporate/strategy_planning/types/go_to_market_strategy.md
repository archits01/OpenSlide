# Go-to-Market Strategy Presentation

**What it is:** A structured plan for entering a new market segment, launching a product, or scaling a GTM motion — covering ICP, channels, messaging, targets, and milestones.

---

## Audience
CEO, board, VP Sales, VP Marketing, and cross-functional GTM leadership. Audience wants confidence in the thesis, clarity on the bet, and specificity on execution.

## Tone
Strategic and analytical. Confident in the opportunity, honest about the risks. Avoid buzzwords — show you've stress-tested the assumptions.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Title** — Product/segment name, GTM motion type, date
2. **The Opportunity** — Market size, segment TAM, why now. Quantified.
3. **Where We've Won So Far** — Current customer base, ARR, key wins that prove the thesis
4. **Target Customer (ICP)** — Firmographics, buying signals, persona, title, pain hierarchy
5. **Why We Win** — Differentiated value proposition in this segment. What we have that competitors don't.
6. **GTM Motion** — Direct sales, PLG, channel, outbound — with rationale for each choice
7. **Messaging Architecture** — Primary message, supporting proof points, objection responses
8. **Channels + Tactics** — Where we find them, how we engage, sequence of touches
9. **Pipeline Model** — Conversion assumptions: leads → qualified → closed. ACV, cycle length, win rate.
10. **Resource Requirements** — Headcount, tools, budget. What we need to hit the targets.
11. **Milestones + Metrics** — 30/60/90-day leading indicators. What success looks like at each stage.
12. **Risks + Mitigations** — Top 3 things that could go wrong and how we'd respond.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Quantified TAM / SAM with sourced data
- Named ICP with buying signal and pain hierarchy — not just company size
- Explicit win rate and conversion assumptions in the pipeline model
- Resource ask tied directly to milestones (headcount, budget)
- Leading indicators for the first 90 days — not just lagging revenue targets
- At least one honest risk with a mitigation

## Must-Avoid
- TAM slides that show the entire market without narrowing to serviceable / obtainable
- GTM "strategy" that is just a list of tactics
- Messaging on a slide that says "value prop: [vague sentence]" — use actual customer language
- Pipeline models without stated assumptions
- Skipping the resource ask — it makes the plan look like a wish list

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Current ARR and customer count in target segment (or closest proxy)
- Total TAM and serviceable TAM ($)
- ACV for new segment, expected sales cycle length
- Target: new logos and ARR in 6/12 months
- CAC assumptions and payback period
- Number of sales reps / SDRs required

## Example Outline (B2B SaaS — Enterprise Segment Entry)

1. Prism Enterprise GTM Strategy: Moving Upmarket
2. The Enterprise Security Market: $2.1B TAM in Our Reach
3. How We've Won in Mid-Market: 340 Customers, $8.2M ARR
4. Enterprise ICP: DevOps-Heavy Companies in Regulated Industries
5. Why Prism Wins Enterprise vs. Alternatives
6. GTM Motion: Direct Sales + Channel Partners
7. Our Enterprise Messaging: From Feature to Risk Reduction
8. Demand Generation: Field, Partner, ABM Channels
9. Pipeline Model: Path to 3 Enterprise Logos in 6 Months
10. What We're Investing: Sales + Marketing Headcount + Budget
11. 90-Day Milestones + Leading Indicators
12. Top 3 Risks + How We'll Respond
