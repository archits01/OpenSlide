# Annual Strategy Presentation

**What it is:** A multi-year strategic plan presented to leadership or the board — covering where the company is, where it's going, and the priorities and investments required to get there.

---

## Audience
Senior leadership team (top 20–50), board of directors, or both. Audience includes leaders who will own execution — frame the strategy as a call to action, not a report.

## Tone
Visionary but grounded. Acknowledge the current reality honestly before painting the future. Framework-driven without being abstract. Every strategic priority should be defensible with evidence.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Title** — Company name, strategy period (e.g. "2025–2027 Strategy"), presenter
2. **Where We Are: Honest Assessment** — Key financial metrics vs. targets. Performance gaps named clearly.
3. **External Context** — Market trends, competitive moves, regulatory changes that constrain or enable strategy.
4. **What Got Us Here Won't Get Us There** — Articulate the strategic inflection point. Why the current approach has limits.
5. **Our Strategic Vision** — Where are we going? Stated simply. Not a mission statement.
6. **Strategic Priorities** — 3–5 named priorities. Each with a one-paragraph rationale and a named owner.
7. **Priority 1: Deep Dive** — What we're doing, investment required, milestones, success metric.
8. **Priority 2: Deep Dive** — Same structure.
9. **Priority 3+: Deep Dive** — Repeat as needed.
10. **Financial Plan** — Revenue targets, investment requirements, efficiency targets by year.
11. **Organizational Capabilities** — What people, skills, and culture changes are required.
12. **Milestones: Year 1 Priorities** — What has to happen in the next 12 months for the strategy to be on track.
13. **The Ask: Commitments from This Room** — Each executive names what they commit to. Accountability.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Honest current state with gaps named — not just strengths
- Named strategic priorities with owners and success metrics
- Financial targets by year with investment requirements
- Year-1 milestones specific enough to be tracked
- An explicit ask from each leader in the room
- One slide on what you're stopping or deprioritizing

## Must-Avoid
- Strategy presentations that are just last year's strategy with updated numbers
- Vision statements without operational translation
- More than 5 strategic priorities — it means there's no real strategy
- Financial slides without investment requirements alongside targets
- Ending without accountability — who owns what

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Current ROE, ROA, or key profitability metric vs. target
- Efficiency ratio or cost structure vs. target
- NPS or customer satisfaction vs. target
- Revenue targets at year 1, 2, 3
- Investment required ($ / headcount) by priority
- Market share or position (current vs. aspiration)

---

## Example Outline (Regional Bank — Senior Leadership Offsite)

1. 2025–2027 Strategy: Building the Next Chapter
2. Where We Are: ROE 9.2%, Efficiency 68%, NPS 41 — Gaps We Must Close
3. The External Environment: Digital Disruption, Margin Compression, Talent War
4. Why We Must Transform: Our Traditional Advantages Are Eroding
5. Our Vision: The Leading Community Bank in the Midwest by 2027
6. Five Strategic Priorities: Digital, Fee Income, Talent, Efficiency, Growth
7. Priority 1: Digital Transformation — Roadmap, Investment, Milestones
8. Priority 2: Fee Income Diversification — New Products, Partnerships
9. Priority 3: Talent & Culture — Hiring, Retention, Capability Building
10. Financial Plan: ROE 12%, Efficiency 62%, NPS 55 by 2027
11. Year 1: What Must Happen Before This Offsite Next Year
12. Commitments from This Leadership Team
