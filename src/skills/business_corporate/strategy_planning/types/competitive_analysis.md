# Competitive Analysis Presentation

**What it is:** A structured evaluation of the competitive landscape — who the key players are, how they compare on dimensions that matter, and where the strategic opportunities and threats lie.

---

## Audience
Strategy, product, and exec leadership. Audience wants insight, not just data. They know the competitors — show them something they didn't already know.

## Tone
Analytical, clear-eyed, and intellectually honest. Don't cherry-pick comparisons to make you look better. Surface uncomfortable truths — credibility comes from objectivity.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Title** — Company name, time period, analyst name
2. **Why This Analysis, Why Now** — What decision or question prompted this. Frames relevance.
3. **Competitive Landscape Map** — Visual positioning of all players on 2 strategic axes. Who is where and why.
4. **Competitors Reviewed** — 4–6 players, one-line summary of each. Include a "rising threat" if applicable.
5. **Comparison Framework** — The dimensions that matter for competitive differentiation. State why these dimensions were chosen.
6. **Competitor Deep Dive #1** — Most important competitor. Strategy, strengths, weaknesses, recent moves, threat level.
7. **Competitor Deep Dive #2** — Second most critical.
8. **Head-to-Head Comparison** — Side-by-side table on key dimensions. Be honest where you lose.
9. **Where We Win / Lose / Co-exist** — Strategic read of the map. Not a feature comparison.
10. **What They're Doing Next** — Signals, announcements, hiring patterns, funding events.
11. **Strategic Implications** — What should we do differently based on this analysis?
12. **Recommendations** — Specific, actionable. Not "monitor the market."

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Positioning map with explicit axis definitions
- At least one honest assessment of where a competitor outperforms you
- NPS or customer satisfaction comparison if available
- Recent moves section — shows the analysis is current
- Strategic implications section — turns data into decisions
- Named dimensions with rationale for why they were chosen

## Must-Avoid
- Comparison tables where you win on every single dimension — unbelievable
- Competitor deep dives that are just copied from their website
- Positioning maps with unlabeled axes
- Analysis without a "so what" — every insight needs an implication
- Including 10+ competitors — narrow to the ones that actually matter

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Market share / revenue estimates where available
- NPS or customer satisfaction scores (own vs. key competitor)
- Pricing comparison (range, model)
- Customer count / growth trajectory
- Funding raised / parent company resources
- Win/loss rate vs. specific competitors (if tracked internally)

---

## Example Outline (UK Challenger Bank)

1. Competitive Analysis: UK Digital Banking Landscape Q2 2025
2. Why Now: Strategy Review Before H2 Investment Decisions
3. The UK Digital Banking Map: Product Breadth vs. Unit Economics
4. Competitors in Scope: Monzo, Revolut, Starling, Lloyds, Nationwide
5. Framework: Product Breadth, CAC, Unit Economics, NPS
6. Monzo Deep Dive: Strengths, NPS 74, Weak on Business Banking
7. Revolut Deep Dive: Product Leader, Unit Economics Risk
8. Head-to-Head: All 5 Players on 6 Dimensions
9. Where We Win, Where We Lose, Where We Differentiate
10. What Competitors Are Doing Next: Recent Signals
11. Strategic Implications: Where to Compete, Where to Yield
12. Recommendations: 3 Moves for H2
