# Scenario Planning Presentation

**What it is:** A structured analysis of multiple plausible futures — typically 2–4 scenarios — with the revenue, cost, and strategic implications of each, used to prepare contingency responses.

---

## Audience
Board, risk committee, or senior executive team. Audience wants to understand exposure and have a response playbook ready. They don't want surprises — they want confidence that leadership has thought this through.

## Tone
Sober, analytical, and decisive. Avoid catastrophizing. Present risks clearly but always pair each scenario with a credible response plan. Tone should be: "We've looked at the hard outcomes — here's how we'd respond."

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Title** — Company name, scenario period (e.g. "18-Month Economic Scenarios"), audience
2. **Why Scenario Planning, Why Now** — What uncertainty is driving this analysis
3. **Scenario Framework** — The primary uncertainty axes. What makes one scenario vs. another.
4. **Scenarios Overview** — One slide showing all scenarios, probability weights, and headline impact
5. **Base Case: [Name]** — Assumptions, key metrics (revenue, EBITDA, headcount), strategic posture
6. **Downside Case: [Name]** — Same structure. What triggers this scenario. Response actions.
7. **Severe/Upside Case: [Name]** — Same structure. Include an upside if relevant to the audience.
8. **Scenario Comparison Table** — Revenue, cost, headcount, cash position across all scenarios
9. **Early Warning Indicators** — What metrics would signal we're heading into each scenario
10. **Pre-Approved Response Playbook** — Actions already authorized for each trigger. Removes decision lag.
11. **Capital and Liquidity Position** — How long can we sustain each scenario without raising capital?
12. **Recommendation + Decision** — Which scenario to plan for operationally. What decisions to make now.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Named scenarios with probability weights
- Revenue and cost impact quantified for each scenario
- Early warning indicators — specific metrics that signal scenario transition
- Pre-approved response actions — not "we will consider" but "we will do X at trigger Y"
- Capital runway / cash position for worst case
- A recommendation — don't just present options, make a call

## Must-Avoid
- Scenarios that are just "optimistic, realistic, pessimistic" without structural differences
- Scenario analysis without stated probability weights
- Response plans that are vague ("reduce costs as needed")
- Omitting the upside scenario if there's a credible one
- Presenting only the downside without the company's resilience — boards need confidence, not just fear

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Revenue impact by scenario: £/$ range
- Cost reduction available: £/$ by category (headcount, discretionary, CapEx)
- Headcount response: how many roles at each stage
- Cash/liquidity runway in worst case
- Early warning metrics and current values vs. thresholds
- Break-even and cash-flow-neutral point in each scenario

---

## Example Outline (Specialist Staffing Company — Board Risk Committee)

1. 18-Month Economic Scenarios: Board Risk Committee Briefing
2. Why Now: Macro Uncertainty Requires Pre-Positioned Responses
3. Our Scenario Framework: Market Volume × Client Demand
4. Three Scenarios: Base, Downside (–15%), Severe (–30%)
5. Base Case: Flat Market — £420M Revenue, No Structural Changes
6. Downside Case: –15% Market Contraction — Revenue Impact, Cost Response
7. Severe Downside: –30% Contraction — Restructuring Response Plan
8. Scenario Comparison: Revenue, EBITDA, Headcount, Cash Position
9. Early Warning Indicators: Metrics We're Watching Now
10. Pre-Approved Response Playbook: Actions at Each Trigger
11. Capital Position: Runway in Each Scenario
12. Recommendation: Plan Operationally for Downside, Stress-Test for Severe
