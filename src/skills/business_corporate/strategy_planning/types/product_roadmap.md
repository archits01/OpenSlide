# Product Roadmap Presentation

**What it is:** A structured, time-horizoned view of what the product team will build and why — designed to align stakeholders, set expectations, and invite feedback.

---

## Audience
Internal exec team, board, customer advisory boards, or key accounts. Adjust depth: customers get themes + outcomes, execs get bets + investment, internal teams get detail.

## Tone
Confident, forward-looking, customer-driven. Grounded in real data (votes, NPS, support tickets, revenue impact). Avoid vague aspirations — every item connects to a business or customer outcome.

---

## Slide Structure

1. **Title** — Presentation name, product name, time period (e.g. "2025 Product Roadmap — Customer Advisory Board")
2. **Where We Are Today** — Key metrics: volume processed, success rate, customer count, ARR. Shows credibility and baseline.
3. **What We Heard From You** — Top requested themes from feedback, support data, NPS verbatims, vote counts. Make customers feel heard.
4. **Our Strategic Bets for [Year]** — 3–5 major themes (not features). Each theme as a named initiative with one-line rationale.
5. **Q1: [Theme Name]** — Specific deliverables, what changes for customers, key metric expected to move.
6. **Q2: [Theme Name]** — Same format. Lead with the problem being solved.
7. **Q3 / H2: [Theme Name]** — Higher-level if further out. Frame as direction, not commitment.
8. **What We're Not Building (Yet)** — Deliberate exclusions with rationale. Shows trade-off thinking.
9. **How We Decide** — Prioritization framework (customer impact × votes × strategic fit). Demystifies decisions.
10. **What We Need From You** — Specific asks: validate priority order, flag missing items, volunteer for beta.
11. **Timeline Summary** — One-slide visual: quarters as columns, initiatives as rows.
12. **Close + Discussion** — Single prompt question to open dialogue.

---

## Must-Have Elements
- Quantified customer pain: vote counts, support ticket volume, resolution time benchmarks
- Business metrics baseline (volume, ARR, success rate) — establishes credibility
- Named themes, not feature lists — roadmaps communicate intent, not specs
- At least one "not building" item — demonstrates prioritization discipline
- Quarter-by-quarter structure with rough H2 direction
- Clear connection between customer feedback and roadmap items

## Must-Avoid
- Feature laundry lists without rationale
- Committing to exact dates in advisory board settings — use quarters
- Marketing language ("revolutionary", "game-changing")
- Slides that are just Gantt charts with no narrative
- Omitting trade-offs — audiences distrust roadmaps showing everything as equal priority

---

## Key Data Points to Include
- Current product metrics: volume, success/uptime rate, ARR, customer count
- Customer feedback signal: vote counts, NPS, support ticket categories
- Industry benchmarks where gaps exist (e.g. "14-day resolution vs. 4-day benchmark")
- Revenue at risk or opportunity tied to top-voted items
- ARR represented in the room (if customer advisory board)

---

## Example Outline (Customer Advisory Board — B2B Payments)

1. 2025 Product Roadmap — Customer Advisory Board
2. Where We Stand: $2.4B volume, 99.3% success rate, 182 customers
3. What You Told Us: Top 5 Requested Themes + Vote Counts
4. Our 4 Strategic Bets for 2025

8. H2: Embedded Financial Services Foundation
9. What We're Deliberately Not Building in 2025
10. How We Prioritize: Our Decision Framework
11. What We Need From This Room
12. Full Timeline — Questions + Discussion
