# Strategy & Planning Presentations

Forward-looking, analytical decks that help leaders make decisions and align organizations on direction. Strategy decks present a point of view — they are not neutral summaries. Every strategy slide should make an argument, not just describe the world.

## Tone

Forward-looking, analytical, confident, and framework-driven. Strategy decks advocate for a position. Use language like "We recommend", "The evidence points to", "The right path is" — not wishy-washy "there are several options to consider." Show your thinking, not just your conclusion.

Avoid: presenting all options as equal (cowardice), pure description without synthesis, lists that read as exhaustive research rather than a recommendation.

Use: pointed conclusions, explicit recommendations, quantified stakes, clear alternatives before the recommendation.

## Design

Use the Corporate design system (see `../shared/design-system.md`). Strategy decks benefit from strong visual frameworks — 3-column pillars, 2×2 matrices (use Comparison Table pattern with a grid structure), roadmaps, and before/after comparisons. Charts should always tell a specific story, not just display data.

## Pattern Priorities

1. **Quarterly Roadmap** (Pattern 6) — for product roadmaps, initiative timelines, OKR planning
2. **Comparison Table** (Pattern 5) — for competitive analysis, scenario comparison, option evaluation, SWOT
3. **3-Column Strategic Pillars** (Pattern 4) — for strategic pillars, OKR structure, GTM pillars, frameworks
4. **Bar Chart + Key Insights** (Pattern 7) — for market sizing, trend analysis, performance benchmarks
5. **Dark Stat Band + Context** (Pattern 3) — for market opportunity, company context, situation framing
6. **KPI Scorecard** (Pattern 2) — for OKR dashboards, scorecard against goals
7. **Executive Cover** (Pattern 1) — for strategy reveal, big announcements
8. **Budget/Investment Breakdown** (Pattern 11) — for resource allocation decisions, investment prioritization
9. **Action Items** (Pattern 12) — always closes a strategy session

## Content Rules

- **Always anchor to business outcome**: "We should launch in APAC" is incomplete. "We should launch in APAC because it represents $400M in addressable market, our competitors have limited presence, and it would add 15% to total ARR by 2027" — that's a strategy argument.
- **Use frameworks explicitly**: Name the framework you're using (SWOT, Porter's 5 Forces, Jobs-to-be-Done, 2×2 prioritization). Frameworks signal analytical rigor and help audiences follow the logic.
- **Show alternatives before recommendation**: Every recommendation should be preceded by "Here are the 3 paths we considered" — then show why the recommendation wins. Never arrive at a conclusion without showing the decision process.
- **Quantify the stakes**: Every strategic choice should state what success looks like ($X revenue, Y% market share, Z-month timeline) and what the cost of inaction is.
- **Be explicit about assumptions**: Every financial projection or market estimate must state its key assumption. "TAM of $12B — assuming 15% annual growth per Gartner 2025 forecast."
- **Use time horizons consistently**: Distinguish between horizon 1 (now–12 months), horizon 2 (1–3 years), and horizon 3 (3+ years) — and label which horizon each initiative lives in.
- **Competitive analysis must have a recommendation**: Don't just map the landscape. Conclude with "Given this competitive position, we recommend..."
- **OKRs must be SMART**: Objectives should be aspirational but clear. Key Results must be numeric with a current state, target state, and deadline. "Improve NPS" is not a Key Result. "Improve NPS from 62 to 75 by December 31, 2026" is.

## Slide-by-Slide Guidance

### GTM Strategy (10–14 slides)
1. Cover (Pattern 1)
2. Market Context (Pattern 3: Dark Stat Band) — market size, growth rate, key trends
3. ICP Definition (Pattern 4: 3-Column Pillars) — Ideal Customer Profile dimensions: firmographic, technographic, behavioral
4. Competitive Landscape (Pattern 5: Comparison Table) — our position vs. 3 competitors
5. Value Proposition (Pattern 4: 3-Column Pillars) — 3 core differentiated value props with evidence
6. Channel Strategy (Pattern 11: Budget Breakdown) — channel mix with pipeline targets
7. Pricing & Packaging (Pattern 2: KPI Scorecard) — 3 tiers with key metrics per tier
8. GTM Roadmap (Pattern 6: Roadmap) — Q1–Q4 milestones by function
9. OKRs (Pattern 2: KPI Scorecard) — 4 headline KRs for GTM
10. Investment Required (Pattern 11: Budget Breakdown) — headcount, marketing, tools
11. Risk & Mitigation (Pattern 5: Comparison Table)
12. Action Items (Pattern 12)

### Competitive Analysis (8–10 slides)
1. Cover (Pattern 1) — "Competitive Intelligence" title, date, audience
2. Competitive Landscape Map (Pattern 5: Comparison Table) — X axis: price, Y axis: capability
3. Feature Comparison (Pattern 5: Comparison Table) — top 8 features, us vs. 3 competitors
4. Win/Loss Analysis (Pattern 7: Bar Chart) — win/loss reasons by competitor
5. Competitive Strengths (Pattern 4: 3-Column Pillars) — our 3 moats
6. Competitive Gaps (Pattern 4: 3-Column Pillars) — 3 areas to close
7. Strategic Response (Pattern 6: Roadmap) — Q1–Q4 product/GTM moves to outcompete
8. Action Items (Pattern 12)

### Annual OKR Planning (8–10 slides)
1. Cover (Pattern 1) — "FY2026 OKR Planning"
2. Last Year in Review (Pattern 3: Dark Stat Band) — 3 headline results vs. prior OKRs
3. Strategic Priorities (Pattern 4: 3-Column Pillars) — 3 company-level objectives
4. Company OKRs (Pattern 2: KPI Scorecard) — 4 key results with current state and target
5. Department Alignment (Pattern 8: Status Dashboard) — each department row with their primary KR
6. H1 Roadmap (Pattern 6: Roadmap) — Q1 and Q2 by team
7. H2 Roadmap (Pattern 6: Roadmap) — Q3 and Q4 by team
8. Resource Allocation (Pattern 11: Budget Breakdown) — investment by priority area
9. Risks & Assumptions (Pattern 5: Comparison Table) — risk, likelihood, impact, mitigation
10. Action Items (Pattern 12) — OKR owners, review cadence, kickoff dates

### Product Roadmap (8–12 slides)
1. Cover (Pattern 1)
2. Vision & Strategy (Pattern 4: 3-Column Pillars) — product vision, north star metric, 3 bets
3. Where We Are Today (Pattern 2: KPI Scorecard) — product metrics: DAU, retention, NPS, revenue
4. What We Learned (Pattern 3: Dark Stat Band) — 3 key insights from last quarter
5. Roadmap Overview (Pattern 6: Roadmap) — Q1–Q4 initiatives with status
6. Theme Deep-Dive (Pattern 4: 3-Column Pillars) — expand on most important theme
7. Tradeoffs (Pattern 5: Comparison Table) — what we're NOT doing and why
8. Success Metrics (Pattern 2: KPI Scorecard) — how we'll know we succeeded
9. Dependencies (Pattern 8: Status Dashboard) — cross-functional dependencies with owners
10. Action Items (Pattern 12)
